\section{First full-phase-space results}
\label{sec:fps}

This section reports the current central value, a three-prior dependence
envelope, and an extension region validated by a hidden-variable closure and
200 coverage toys.  The former full systematic covariance and unified-throw
adoption are withdrawn while the corrected uncertainty production is run;
uncertainty-derived FPS comparisons are not quoted in the interim.

The published phase space ($\theta_\mu<20^{\circ}$, $1.5<\pPar<\SI{60}{GeV/c}$,
$\pT<\SI{4.5}{GeV/c}$) hides a third of the signal: weighting the true
$\nu_\mu$~CC rate in the tracker fiducial volume, \SI{66.4}{\percent} lies
inside the cuts, \SI{22.3}{\percent} at $\pPar<\SI{1.5}{GeV/c}$,
\SI{11.3}{\percent} at $\theta_\mu>20^{\circ}$, and the high-$\pT$/$\pPar$
tails are negligible (Fig.~\ref{fig:fpsacc}). Because OmniFold's miss
treatment performs acceptance correction natively, removing the truth-level
kinematic cuts is a configurational change (an event-loop switch enlarging
the truth denominator from 32.8M to 49M events; the reconstruction-level
selection, including the MINOS match, is untouched), and the unfold
extrapolates into the unmeasured regions through the step-1 miss regressor.
The question is not \emph{whether} it can extrapolate but \emph{at
what prior cost}; this section quantifies that.

\paragraph{Validation battery.}
Three results establish the extension (Fig.~\ref{fig:fpscmp}):
\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Anchor.} Restricted to the published-phase-space sub-block,
        the full-phase-space unfold reproduces a control unfold (standard
        omnifile, paper grid, identical settings) to $0.9994$ in integral,
        with a median per-cell ratio of $1.0013$ and median deviation
        \SI{\fpsAnchor}{\percent} over all 185 reported cells --- opening the
        phase space does not damage the measurement where it was already
        made. The control itself reproduces the frozen production total
        ($\SI{\sigTwoD}{cm^2/nucleon}$) exactly.
  \item \textbf{Closure.} A self-closure on the extended grid (simulated
        reconstruction as pseudo-data) returns recovered/truth $=1.0000$ in
        every cell including the extension --- no bookkeeping or
        normalization bias in the enlarged-denominator configuration. A
        \emph{hidden-variable} closure goes further: injecting a Gaussian
        truth bump in true $E_{\mathrm{avail}}$ (an axis the
        $(\pT,\pPar)$ unfold is blind to) and unfolding, the recovered
        $(\pT,\pPar)$ cross section reproduces the bump-reweighted truth to
        median \SI{0.17}{\percent} (90th percentile \SI{0.65}{\percent}) in
        the published cells and median \SI{0.77}{\percent} (max
        \SI{4.05}{\percent}) in the extension --- both well inside the
        tier-2 prior band.
  \item \textbf{Three-prior envelope.} Repeating the unfold with three
        priors --- MnvTune~v1, bare GENIE, and a NuWro-shaped prior (built
        by reweighting the truth $(\pT,\pPar)$ shape to two million raw
        NuWro events, which carry no phase-space cut) --- spans the total
        cross section by only $\pm\SI{1.5}{\percent}$. Per cell, the
        half-spread/mean is median \SI{2.9}{\percent} inside the published
        region but median \SI{7.9}{\percent} (90th percentile
        \SI{62}{\percent}) in the extension, concentrated in the
        zero-acceptance cells (Fig.~\ref{fig:fpsenv}).
  \item \textbf{Coverage.} 200 closure$+$bootstrap toys give per-bin
        coverage (fraction of toys with $|r|\le1$) of mean
        \SI{68.9}{\percent} against the \SI{68.27}{\percent} target
        ($\langle|r|\rangle=0.79$, target $0.80$); the published
        (\SI{68.5}{\percent}) and extension (\SI{70.0}{\percent}, marginally
        conservative) regions are both correctly calibrated --- the bootstrap
        band means what it says in the extended grid.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Result and reporting convention.}
The full-phase-space CC-inclusive cross section on the tracker fiducial
volume is
\begin{equation*}
  \sigma_{\mathrm{FPS}} = \SI{\fpsSigma}{cm^2/nucleon}
  \quad\text{(central value)},
\end{equation*}
\SI{\fpsAbove}{\percent} above the restricted-phase-space
$\SI{\sigTwoD}{cm^2/nucleon}$. We adopt \textbf{two-tier reporting}:
\emph{tier~1} (the measurement) covers cells with $\epsilon\gtrsim2\%$ ---
the published region plus the measurable extension ($\sim\SI{6}{\percent}$
of the rate, chiefly the $\theta>20^{\circ}$ wedge and the
$\pPar\in[0.75,1.5]\,\si{GeV/c}$ edge); \emph{tier~2} (prior-extrapolated)
covers the dead cells ($\sim\SI{28}{\percent}$ of the rate), published but
flagged, carrying the three-prior envelope as an explicit additional band.

The FPS central value, restricted-region anchor, and three-prior envelope remain
current. Its former universe/bootstrap/ML budget and unified-throw adoption used
the superseded one-sided, CV-centered, jitter-subtracted construction and are
unquotable. Corrected FPS uncertainty production must use the contract in
\S\ref{sec:uq-combine} and selection-complete lateral support appropriate to
the enlarged phase space. No replacement uncertainty magnitude is asserted
here.

\subsection{The point-cloud realization: full statistics on the 5D grid}
\label{sec:fps-pet}
The result above establishes the full-phase-space extension on the
two-dimensional $(\pT,\pPar)$ scalar grid. The point-cloud track
(\S\ref{sec:pet}) repeats the exercise on the full 5D headline grid
$(\pT,\pPar,\Eavail,q_3,W)$, with two differences from the GBDT case. First,
because OmniFold's step-2 network is a learned function of the truth phase
space it was trained on, opening the kinematic cuts requires a genuine PET
retrain on the enlarged truth sample rather than a reweight of the
restricted-phase-space network. Second, because retraining separately for
each prior hypothesis is prohibitively expensive for this network, the
model-dependence envelope reuses the frozen-reweighter transfer that
underlies the PET systematic budget (\S\ref{sec:pet-syst}): a cheap GBDT
re-unfold under three priors --- MnvTune~v1, bare GENIE, and the NuWro-shaped
prior --- supplies per-bin fractional prior-dependence ratios, applied to the
frozen PET headline weights rather than driving three additional PET
trainings.

\paragraph{Extending the grid and the truth phase space.}
Two lifts are applied in sequence, each validated by re-training PET on the
enlarged sample: (1)~the $\theta_\mu<20^\circ$ truth gate is lifted
consistently across the point-cloud dump, the GBDT dump, and the PET retrain,
enlarging the truth sample from \num{32.9}~million to \num{38.6}~million
events; (2)~the reporting grid is then extended with catch bins for the
low-$\pPar$ strip and the high-$\pT$ corner ($\pT$: 15 bins, $\pPar$: 19 bins,
against the published $14\times16$), giving a final
$15\times19\times7\times7\times6=\num{83790}$-bin grid on
\num{49152885}~truth events. Restricting the extended-grid PET cross section
to the \num{9752}~bins fully interior to the published $\theta_\mu<20^\circ$
rectangle reproduces the restricted-phase-space PET headline to integral
ratio $0.99$, median per-bin deviation \SI{1.6}{\percent} (90th percentile
\SI{4.2}{\percent}) --- consistent with ordinary retrain-to-retrain noise
between two independently-trained networks (quantified directly below), not a
distortion from the extended grid or the lifted gate.

\paragraph{Model-dependence envelope and two-tier reporting.}
Of the \num{11875}~bins the PET headline populates on the extended grid,
\num{9307} clear a reconstruction-efficiency completeness of $\ge0.5$
(tier~1, measured) and \num{2568} fall below it (tier~2, extrapolated),
concentrated in the low-$\pPar$ catch rows and the high-$\pT$ column. The
three-prior model-dependence envelope (half-spread/mean per bin) has median
\SI{18.7}{\percent} (90th percentile \SI{61.6}{\percent}) in tier~1 and
median \SI{16.3}{\percent} (90th percentile \SI{45.4}{\percent}) in tier~2
--- flat across the completeness range at large, rather than blooming in the
extrapolated cells the way the coarser 2D pilot's efficiency-binned
prior-swap did (that comparison used two priors and a different disagreement
metric on the $14\times16$ grid; the finer 5D binning combined with the
three-prior GBDT transfer evidently constrains the catch bins comparably well
to the interior). The combined model-dependence covariance has
$\sqrt{\mathrm{tr}\,C_{\text{modeldep}}} = \num{8.56e-37}$; because
$C_{\text{modeldep}}$ is rank~2 by construction (built from two
prior-difference vectors), it is stored and combined as those two vectors
rather than as a dense matrix.

\paragraph{Retraining-response floor.}
The flat 16--19\% envelope raises an obvious question: how much of that is a
genuine three-prior disagreement, and how much is ordinary PET training
stochasticity? To isolate the latter, six additional PET replicas were
trained varying only the random seed and the training-sample size (four
replicas at 10M events, two at 5M), evaluated through the identical
extraction chain, and their per-bin spread compared against the
model-dependence envelope in each tier. At 10M events the tier-2
retraining-response floor is median \SI{1.14}{\percent} (90th percentile
\SI{2.17}{\percent}), a factor of $\sim14$ below the \SI{16.3}{\percent}
tier-2 envelope, and it shrinks further from the 5M-event value
(\SI{1.37}{\percent}), the expected direction for genuine convergence rather
than an already-saturated noise floor. Retraining noise is therefore a
negligible contributor next to the three-prior systematic, justifying the
frozen-reweighter transfer used for both the model-dependence envelope above
and the PET systematic budget (\S\ref{sec:pet-syst}): training PET once and
propagating prior/systematic variation through fractional reweighting, rather
than retraining per universe, does not discard resolving power that a full
per-universe retrain would have recovered.
