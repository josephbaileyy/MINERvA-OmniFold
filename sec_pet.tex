\section{Point-cloud track: PET on the raw reconstructed clusters}
\label{sec:pet}

The full promise of OmniFold is unfolding on the event's \emph{raw}
representation rather than on engineered scalar summaries. This section
documents that track: a point-cloud transformer (PET, the architecture of
OmniLearn~\cite{Mikuni:2024qsr}, vendored with the upstream OmniFold
implementation) unfolds the same ME~FHC data using, on the reconstructed
side, the actual calorimetric recoil clusters --- per-cluster energy,
transverse position and $z$ (\texttt{cluster\_energy}, \texttt{cluster\_pos},
\texttt{cluster\_z}, filtered on \texttt{cluster\_isMuontrack} via
\texttt{CVUniverse::GetRecoClusters()}) alongside the muon kinematics. An
earlier attempt that read the wrong, mostly-empty
\texttt{ExtraEnergyClusters\_*} branches was discarded and the chain rebuilt
from the corrected dump. \textbf{Maturity:} this is a validated \emph{method
milestone} --- a real, absolutely-normalised, full-statistics cross section
with its own closure gate and a first systematic budget --- but it is not yet
at the production GBDT result's maturity (its training is
subsample-limited; see below).

\subsection{From shape check to absolute cross section}
\label{sec:pet-abs}
The PET network trains on a GPU on a 2M-event subsample (2 OmniFold
iterations); the learned step-2 truth reweight is then applied to the
\emph{full} 32.8M-event generator sample
(\texttt{MultiFold.reweight}), binned on the 4D grid, and pushed through the
standard extraction (\texttt{extract\_cross\_section\_nd} with the GBDT
run's completeness --- completeness is reweight-independent --- and the same
flux/POT/nucleon constants). Three gates validate the absolute path:
\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Full-statistics reweight sanity}: the pushed weights over the
        32.8M generator events are finite with mean $1.028$;
  \item \textbf{Closure (decisive)}: unfolding MC-reco-as-pseudo-data
        recovers MC truth to total $0.9884$ with uniform per-axis median
        residuals of $\sim\SI{1.1}{\percent}$ --- the absolute machinery is
        unbiased;
  \item \textbf{Cross-check vs GBDT}: on data, the PET absolute total is
        $\SI{2.796e-38}{cm^2/nucleon}$ versus the GBDT
        $\SI{3.066e-38}{}$, ratio $\petRatio$, with per-axis median differences
        of \SIrange{6.5}{9.9}{\percent} (Fig.~\ref{fig:petabs}).
\end{enumerate}
Because the closure is exact to $\sim\SI{\petClosure}{\percent}$, the $\sim\SI{\petGbdtGap}{\percent}$
data-side gap is a genuine \emph{training-configuration} difference (PET
trained on the 2M subsample with 2 iterations versus the full-statistics
5-iteration GBDT), not a normalisation bug: an under-iterated unfold pushes
the result less far from the prior. A higher-epoch retrain moved the total by
$-1.6\%$ (ratio $0.897$), confirming the training-config sensitivity; closing
the gap toward the ML band needs more events/iterations and is deferred.

\subsection{PET systematic budget (frozen reweighter)}
\label{sec:pet-syst}
A first full uncertainty budget is attached with the \emph{frozen-reweighter}
method: the PET step-2 weights are held fixed while each systematic
universe's truth reweight ratios re-bin the generator sample --- 13 knob
bands, the 100-universe flux multisim, a 100-replica Poisson bootstrap
(statistical), an alternative-training difference (ML), and the detector
(lateral) bands transferred from the GBDT 4D covariance by fractional
projection. The combined budget on the PET reported bins has median
per-bin fractions of \SI{8.2}{\percent} (systematic), \SI{4.2}{\percent}
(statistical), \SI{3.3}{\percent} (ML), \SI{4.0}{\percent} (lateral,
transferred), totalling $\approx\SI{12.3}{\percent}$ --- comparable to the
GBDT 4D budget's \SI{13.5}{\percent}.

A bin-by-bin matched comparison turns this budget-total statement into a
direct answer to whether the point cloud \emph{reduces} the uncertainty or
merely \emph{agrees with} the scalars. Both engines' reported bins map back
onto the common $14\times16\times7\times7$
$(p_T,p_\parallel,E_\text{avail},q_3)$ grid; the \petCommonBins{} PET bins are
a strict subset of the GBDT's (every PET bin is GBDT-reported, the GBDT
reporting \petGbdtExtra{} additional bins). On the common bins the median
per-bin fractional uncertainty is \SI{\petMedianFracBin}{\percent} for PET ---
here using the \emph{independence-fair} PET-native lateral block
(\SI{1.7}{\percent}) rather than the GBDT-transferred one --- against
\SI{\petGbdtMedianFracBin}{\percent} for the GBDT, a median per-bin ratio of
$\petGbdtRatioBin$ with PET the tighter of the two in
\SI{\petBinsTighter}{\percent} of bins (Fig.~\ref{fig:petunc}). The point
cloud therefore \emph{matches} the scalar precision rather than reducing it:
it tracks the GBDT across the populated kinematics and is wider only in the
sparse extreme-momentum catch bins, where the subsample-limited PET
statistics dominate (Fig.~\ref{fig:petuncratio}). The conclusion is robust to
the lateral treatment --- the more conservative GBDT-transferred lateral lifts
the PET median only to $\approx\SI{12}{\percent}$ --- but is, like the rest of
this section, \emph{indicative}: the budget is anchored to the 2M-event
training and a full-statistics PET is expected to tighten it.

The reweight bank underlying the
systematic block is built from the truth-only miss-row dump, with per-event
alignment verified bit-identical over all \num{32.8}~million rows and the
bank-independent statistical and ML blocks reproducing as a control. The
residual gap to a fully self-contained PET budget is
per-lateral re-inference on shifted reconstructed clouds, which requires
re-running the PET network per lateral universe and is deferred.

\subsection{Truth-cloud projection: one unfold, many hadronic observables}
\label{sec:pet-projection}
\bpn{Can you do point-cloud $\to$ point-cloud --- i.e.\ unfold once to a
truth-level cloud you can then project any 2--5D cross section from?}
The truth side of the PET unfold is \emph{already} a point cloud: the
generator-level final-state hadron set
(\texttt{part\_gen}, the top-12-by-energy truth hadrons with their PDG codes,
built by \texttt{CVUniverse::GetTruthFSHadrons}) is carried per event
alongside the learned step-2 push weight. \emph{Any} truth observable that is
a function of those 4-vectors can therefore be obtained \emph{after} the
unfold by re-binning the push-weighted truth events --- no re-training and no
re-unfolding. We demonstrate this \emph{reweight-then-project} capability on
three observables computed post-hoc from the stored cloud: available energy
$\Eavail$, the hadronic-system invariant mass
$W_{\text{had}}=\sqrt{(\sum E)^2-|\sum\vec p|^2}$, and the proton/charged-pion
multiplicities. The exercise also lets us quantify, honestly, the two real
limitations of the stored cloud.

\paragraph{Truncation is essentially lossless.}
Because $\Eavail$ recomputed from the cloud uses the \emph{same} formula and
inputs as the value stored at dump time, the cloud-vs-stored residual isolates
the cost of keeping only the 12 highest-energy hadrons. Over the full
\num{32.8}~million truth events (every event now carries a cloud, below) the
residual has median
\SI{0}{MeV} and mean \SI{\pcEavailMean}{MeV}, with \SI{\pcEavailWithin}{\percent}
of events within \SI{10}{MeV} (Fig.~\ref{fig:pcvalid}). The entire deficit
lives in the \SI{\pcSaturated}{\percent} of events that saturate the 12-particle
cap (median deficit \SI{\pcSatDeficit}{MeV} there, exactly zero below it), and
because the ranking is by energy the dropped constituents are the softest
mesons --- a small, one-sided, bounded effect. \jrb{So the top-12 truncation
the cloud worried us about is a non-issue at $\Eavail$ scale; raising
\texttt{num\_part} is not warranted.}

\paragraph{Coverage: a targeted re-dump closes the acceptance gap.}
Originally the cloud was filled only in the reco-signal event loop, so the
truth-only ``miss'' events appended for the unfold (\SI{\pcMiss}{\percent} of
the truth sample) carried no cloud --- only \SI{\pcHasCloudBefore}{\percent} of
truth events had one, and cloud projections were correspondingly
\emph{acceptance-limited}, landing exactly on the stored-$\Eavail$ projection
\emph{restricted to the has-cloud subset} but sitting $1.5$--$2\times$ below
the full unfold per bin. This is a truth-side instance of the same
dangling-miss-row dump issue tracked for the systematic bank. We fixed it at
source by carrying the truth final-state hadrons through the
truth-denominator loop and filling \texttt{part\_gen} for the appended miss
rows (the reco cloud \texttt{part\_reco} correctly stays empty for a miss),
then re-dumping, rebuilding the inputs, and re-training. Coverage is now
complete: \SI{\pcHasCloud}{\percent} of truth events carry a cloud (only
\pcEmptyCloud{} residual empties out of \num{32.8}~million), and the
cloud-recomputed $d\sigma/d\Eavail$ now reproduces the \emph{full} unfold to
$\lesssim\SI{\pcCloudFull}{\percent}$ in every $\Eavail$ bin
(Fig.~\ref{fig:pcxsec}, centre, where the cloud, stored, and full-unfold
curves coincide). The projection is no longer acceptance-limited.

\paragraph{Projection yields genuinely new observables.}
$W_{\text{had}}$ is \emph{not} a lossy copy of the leptonic ``experimenter's''
$W$ stored at dump time --- the latter is reconstructed from muon kinematics
assuming a single struck nucleon at rest, while $W_{\text{had}}$ is the true
invariant mass of the hadron system. The two differ by a median
\SI{\pcWoffset}{GeV}, and the 2D correlation
(Fig.~\ref{fig:pcxsec}, left) makes the physics explicit: a sharp locus at
$W_{\text{had}}\approx\SI{0.94}{GeV}$ (single-baryon final states, whose
hadronic mass is just the nucleon rest mass irrespective of leptonic $W$) and
diagonal bands stepped above it by $\sim$one nucleon mass per extra final-state
baryon (multi-nucleon knockout, 2p2h), Fermi-smeared. Projecting the cloud
thus delivers a new, physically meaningful hadronic observable from the
existing unfold.

\paragraph{The OmniFold caveat (and what would need a re-dump).}
\label{sec:pet-projection-next}
Reweighting can only redistribute weight among truth configurations the
generator already produced; it cannot populate phase space the prior never
sampled --- the same extrapolation limit flagged for the full-phase-space
measurement (\S\ref{sec:fps}). Within that limit, every observable above is
now projectable over the \emph{full} truth spectrum from the current dump.
Two things would still need a further re-dump rather than a re-projection:
adding the muon back as a tagged constituent for muon-dependent hadronic
observables (it is deliberately excluded from the recoil cloud but retained as
a stored scalar); and raising the 12-particle cap for the rare
very-high-multiplicity tail (unnecessary at $\Eavail$ scale per the validation
above). \jrb{Bottom line: ``unfold once, project many hadronic cross
sections'' now works over the full truth spectrum --- the miss-row coverage
gap that previously confined it to the reconstructed-and-accepted subset has
been fixed at source and the cloud $d\sigma/d\Eavail$ reproduces the full
unfold to better than \SI{\pcCloudFull}{\percent} per bin.} Full details and
reproduction are in
\texttt{nd-unfolding/pet/POINTCLOUD\_PROJECTION.md}.

\subsection{What the PET track demonstrates}
The point-cloud track closes the methodological loop begun by the scalar
GBDT/MLP cross-checks (\S\ref{sec:validation}): the unfolded physics is
stable across classifier family (GBDT, MLP, transformer) \emph{and} input
representation (engineered scalars, raw reconstructed clusters), now
including the absolute normalisation. It also establishes the
infrastructure --- point-cloud DataLoader, GPU training, full-sample
reweighting, frozen-reweighter budgets --- for unfolding directly on
low-level MINERvA data, the natural endpoint of the unbinned program.
Finally, because the truth side is itself a point cloud, a single unfold
yields not one cross section but a reusable truth object from which any
hadronic observable can be projected after the fact
(\S\ref{sec:pet-projection}).
