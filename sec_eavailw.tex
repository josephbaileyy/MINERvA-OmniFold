¥section{Fifth axis: the $(¥Eavail,W)$ deep-inelastic-tail study}
¥label{sec:eavailw}

Enabling Valencia 2p2h resolves the low-recoil excess (¥S¥ref{sec:3d-2p2h})
but leaves a separate $+2.2¥sigma$ data excess in the
$¥Eavail¥in[1.50,3.00)¥,¥si{GeV}$ bin, where 2p2h does not contribute and the
sub-percent pion-FSI dials cannot reach. Quantified against the adopted
combined 4D covariance (¥S¥ref{sec:4d}) marginalised to the $¥Eavail$ axis,
the discrepancy is severe: GENIE‾CV, GENIE$+$MEC, NuWro and GiBUU are
incompatible with the unfolded $d¥sigma/d¥Eavail$ at $>21¥sigma$ over the
seven $¥Eavail$ bins ($22.4¥sigma$, $24.9¥sigma$, $21.9¥sigma$, $21.2¥sigma$
respectively) and at $>15¥sigma$ in the DIS tail
($¥Eavail¥ge¥SI{0.8}{GeV}$) alone. GiBUU spreads its deficit across the entire
tail (data/generator $1.59$ at $¥Eavail¥in[0.8,1.5)$, $1.36$ at $[1.5,3.0)$)
rather than piling it in the deep catch bin like the GENIE variants --- an
independent, qualitatively distinct confirmation. To test the deep-inelastic
hypothesis directly, we added the hadronic invariant mass $W$ as a fifth
OmniFold axis.

¥subsection{$W$ as a fifth axis}
¥label{sec:eavailw-axis}
Truth $W$ is the experimenter's invariant mass
$W=¥sqrt{M^{2}+2M(E_¥nu-E_¥mu)-Q^{2}}$ with $M$ the struck-nucleon mass
(¥texttt{CVUniverse::GetTrueExperimentersW()}); reco $W$ uses the same
calorimetric energy transfer $q_{0}$ and muon-kinematic $Q^{2}$ as the reco
$q_{3}$ estimator, with the average nucleon mass
(¥texttt{CVUniverse::RecoW()}). Like $q_{3}$ --- and unlike $¥Eavail$ --- $W$
is not invariant under the lateral systematic shifts, so the event loop dumps
shifted-$W$ lateral universes per universe, mirroring the $q_{3}$ treatment
(¥S¥ref{sec:4d}); the dump also carries truth hadronic diagnostics (proton and
pion multiplicities, leading-hadron angle). The $W$
binning $[0,1.1,1.4,1.8,2.2,3.0,100]¥,¥si{GeV}$ brackets the quasielastic
peak, the $¥Delta$, the transition region, and the DIS tail. The five-axis
unfold $(¥pT,¥pPar,¥Eavail,q_{3},W)$ passes the same marginalisation anchors
as its predecessors: the $W$-marginal recovers the frozen 4D cross section to
¥SI{¥eavailMargin}{¥percent} in normalization (5D/4D total $=1.0011$), with per-axis
median differences of ¥SIrange{0.3}{1.5}{¥percent}, and the
$d¥sigma/dW$ spectrum is finite and non-negative across all six bins.

¥subsection{Where the excess lives in the $(¥Eavail,W)$ plane}
¥label{sec:eavailw-map}
Projecting the unfolded five-axis result onto $(¥Eavail,W)$ and subtracting
the GENIE central value localises the excess
(Fig.‾¥ref{fig:eavailWexcess}): it is ¥emph{predominantly deep-inelastic}.
The two highest-$¥Eavail$ bands carry ¥SI{57}{¥percent} of the positive
excess; of all positive excess, high-$¥Eavail$ ($¥ge¥SI{0.8}{GeV}$) holds
¥SI{67}{¥percent}, and ¥SI{83}{¥percent} of ¥emph{that} sits at
$W¥ge¥SI{1.8}{GeV}$, with the single largest cell the deep-DIS corner

¥subsection{Generator band: the corner is generator- and tune-robust}
¥label{sec:eavailw-band}
We confirm this against independent spline-normalised $(¥Eavail,W)$
predictions from GENIE‾CV, GENIE$+$Valencia‾2p2h/MEC, NuWro, and GiBUU
(Fig.‾¥ref{fig:eavailWband}). All four underpredict the
high-$¥Eavail¥times$high-$W$ corner by ¥SIrange{54}{58}{¥percent}

does ¥emph{not} close the corner --- it slightly worsens it ($1.54¥to1.58$),

generators miss it by the same or a larger margin (GiBUU is the most
deficient overall, integrated $¥sigma=¥SI{2.22e-38}{cm^2/nucleon}$ against
the data's ¥SI{3.07e-38}{}); at $W¥in[2.2,3.0)¥,¥si{GeV}$ all four sit

¥subsection{The $(¥Eavail,W)$ covariance and significances}
¥label{sec:eavailw-cov}
To turn the band into a number we build a dedicated 42-bin $(¥Eavail,W)$
covariance, $C = C_{¥mathrm{syst}}+C_{¥mathrm{stat}}+C_{¥mathrm{lateral}}$,
by a ¥emph{frozen-reweighter} block sum on the merged five-axis
¥texttt{¥_universes¥_full} sample: the central-value OmniFold push-weights
are held fixed and only the per-event truth reweight ratios change per
systematic universe (the same methodology as the PET budget,
¥S¥ref{sec:pet-syst}; no per-universe re-inference). The covariance carries
13 knob bands $¥pm1¥sigma$, the 100-universe flux multisim, a Poisson
statistical diagonal, and a $W$-resolved detector (lateral) block. The
laterals are computed directly rather than transferred: the event loop dumps
shifted-$(¥pT,¥pPar,q_3,W)$ universes for the six muon/beam laterals
(¥texttt{Muon¥_Energy¥_MINERvA/MINOS}, ¥texttt{MuonResolution},
¥texttt{MinosEfficiency}, ¥texttt{BeamAngleX/Y}) plus three GEANT weight bands
(¥texttt{GEANT¥_Neutron/Pion/Proton}), each re-inferred on the five-axis grid,
so the $(¥Eavail,W)$ lateral block is built in place. The $W$-resolved block
(median ¥SI{2.36}{¥percent} per bin, $¥sqrt{¥mathrm{tr}}=¥num{9.52e-40}$) is
¥emph{larger} than the earlier 4D-marginalised approximation
(¥SI{1.80}{¥percent}, $¥num{7.99e-40}$) and is the one adopted here. The
result has a median relative uncertainty of ¥SI{14.9}{¥percent} per bin and is
validated by reproducing the frozen five-axis product's $¥Eavail$ and $W$
marginals (sweep central value vs.¥ frozen central value,
$¥max|¥text{ratio}-1|=0.007$).
