BUILD 1
Speaker move: Inventory the inputs: measured reco events and paired truth/reco simulation, including misses.
Purpose: Makes clear which information is—and is not—available in data.

BUILD 2
Speaker move: Push the current truth weights through the simulated pairs to reconstructed space.
Purpose: Introduces “push” as bookkeeping through a known simulated event identity.

BUILD 3
Speaker move: Step 1: compare weighted reconstructed simulation with data.
Purpose: Shows exactly where the data constrain the weights.

BUILD 4
Speaker move: Pull the learned detector-space correction back through each pair.
Purpose: Shows the crucial reco-to-truth information path and the native miss treatment.

BUILD 5
Speaker move: Step 2: learn a smooth truth-space reweight that becomes the next push.
Purpose: Corrects the common oversimplification that OmniFold merely copies weights once.

BUILD 6
Speaker move: Iterate until changes are small; retain the weighted truth ensemble.
Purpose: Ends on the actual product and identifies iteration/model capacity as regularization.
