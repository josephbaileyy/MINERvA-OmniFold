\section{Validation}
\label{sec:validation}

\paragraph{Closure.}
Three closure families, all passing (1A lgbm 5-iter; thresholds in parentheses):
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Truth-reweight} (multiply truth and reco weights by the same
        factor): gaussian-$\pT$ residual \SI{0.046}{\percent}, tilt-$\pPar$
        \SI{0.013}{\percent} (thr \SI{1.5}{\percent}) --- injected truth recovered.
  \item \textbf{Hidden-variable} (bias on $\Delta\pT=\pT^{\mathrm{reco}}-\pT^{\mathrm{truth}}$,
        a resolution variable not in the feature set): leakage linear in amplitude,
        $A=0.05/0.10/0.30\to0.57/1.14/3.43\,\%$ (thr \SI{3.0}{\percent}).
  \item \textbf{Alt-model} (train on CV, pseudo-data from an alternative universe):
        MaCCQE \SI{0.068}{\percent}, Flux \SI{0.376}{\percent} (thr \SI{2.0}{\percent}).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Bottom-line test.}
Following the unbinned-unfolding guide~\cite{unbinnedguide}, the unfolded result must
recover the data's deviation from the prior with a residual far smaller than the feature
itself (the unfolding cannot manufacture discriminating power). Using the closure runs,
in the bins carrying the injected feature the unfolded result tracks the injected truth to
$\approx10\times$ better than the feature size: 2D (gaussian-bump truth reweight) residual
\SI{1.69}{\percent} vs injected \SI{17.2}{\percent} (ratio $0.098$); 3D (+\SI{30}{\percent}
$\Eavail$ bump, $\Eavail$ projection) \SI{1.84}{\percent} vs \SI{18.1}{\percent} (ratio
$0.102$). Both pass.
(The naive data-vs-prior form---reco vs truth $\chi^2$---instead has truth$>$reco, as
expected for a high-resolution measurement where detector smearing lowers the reco baseline
and a stat-only diagonal omits the unfolding covariance; the proper full-covariance
goodness-of-fit is the $\chi^2$-vs-paper of \S\ref{sec:results-tension} and the generator
comparisons.)

\paragraph{Unbinned goodness-of-fit.}
As a binning-free check that the unfold actually removes the data\,$\leftrightarrow$\,MC
mismatch, we use a classifier two-sample test (C2ST): a classifier is trained to separate the
data from the (reweighted) simulation in the full unbinned feature space, and its
test-set accuracy is compared to the chance value $0.5$. \emph{Before} unfolding the two are
separable --- accuracy $0.523$, AUC $0.535$, $z=33.4$ ($p\approx5\times10^{-244}$);
\emph{after} unfolding the classifier is driven to chance --- accuracy $0.501$, AUC $0.501$,
$z=1.4$, $p=0.17$. Data and unfolded simulation are thus statistically indistinguishable with
no detectable residual structure: the unbinned analogue of a binned goodness-of-fit, and it
passes.

\paragraph{Coverage.}
200 closure$+$bootstrap toys: mean coverage \SI{68.71}{\percent} vs Gaussian
\SI{68.27}{\percent}; mean $|\mathrm{residual}|/\sigma$ $0.794$ vs
$\sqrt{2/\pi}=0.798$; \SI{97.6}{\percent} of bins meet the \SI{65}{\percent}
target. The bootstrap band is well-calibrated.

\paragraph{Stability.}
5-iter total $\sigma$ within \SI{0.026}{\percent} of 10-iter (per-bin shape RMS
\SI{1.54}{\percent}); HistGBT and exact-GBT agree on total $\sigma$ to
\SI{0.04}{\percent}. Efficiency (Fig.~\ref{fig:eff}) reproduces the published
Fig.~5.

\paragraph{Classifier choice (GBDT vs.\ NN).}
Published OmniFold analyses use neural-network
classifiers~\cite{Andreassen:2019cjw,unbinnedguide}; we use gradient-boosted decision
trees, whose output must still be a calibrated posterior for $w=p/(1-p)$ to be valid.
On the step-1 reco classifier (data vs.\ MC reco) the reliability curve is near-diagonal
(Brier \(\approx0.25\) at AUC \(\approx0.537\): data and reco MC are nearly
indistinguishable, so the reweight is a modest correction). Across the
$(\pT,\pPar)$ bins the true data/MC ratio still spans \(\approx\SI{100}{\percent}\);
the GBDT recovers it to \SI{4.7}{\percent} median, at least as well as a dense MLP
(\SI{20.9}{\percent}, untuned), and the two reweights agree with bin-wise correlation
$0.92$ --- the learned reweight is robust to the classifier family. Beyond scalar
classifiers, the vendored Point-Edge Transformer (PET~\cite{Mikuni:2024qsr}) run on the
\emph{per-hadron point clouds} --- the variable-length sets of reconstructed energy clusters
and truth final-state hadrons, which a GBDT structurally cannot ingest --- reproduces the
scalar-GBDT 4D unfold to \SIrange{2}{4}{\percent} median per bin on all four axes
($\pT$ \SI{3.9}{}, $\pPar$ \SI{2.3}{}, $\Eavail$ \SI{1.9}{}, $q_{3}$ \SI{2.8}{\percent};
Fig.~\ref{fig:petgbdt}). The method therefore extends to set-valued inputs without changing
the result at these scalar features, the prerequisite for a future point-cloud measurement of
a genuinely hadronic observable.
