\section{Uncertainty quantification}
\label{sec:systematics}

The uncertainty budget combines three independent blocks, summed assuming
independence (distinct RNGs / physics sources). Full procedures are in
\texttt{uq\_statistical\_methods.tex} (App.~\ref{app:methods}); the headline is
that our \emph{standalone} combined budget matches the published total.

\paragraph{Systematic universes.}
44 bands (6 lateral, 38 vertical), 187 universes, reweighted/re-run via the
MAT-MINERvA universe machinery; the covariance is built MAT-conformant
(\texttt{MnvVertErrorBand::CalcCovMx}; verified byte-for-byte against MAT
\texttt{MnvH1D::GetTotalErrorMatrix}, max rel.\ diff $\sim10^{-17}$). Top bands:
Flux \SI{4.99}{\percent}, Muon\_Energy\_MINOS \SI{2.31}{\percent},
Muon\_Energy\_MINERvA \SI{1.29}{\percent}, MinosEfficiency \SI{1.47}{\percent}.

\paragraph{Flux $1/\Phi$ normalisation.}
The dominant flux term enters as $\sigma\propto1/\Phi$; varying $\Phi$ per flux
universe (rather than dividing every universe by the CV $\Phi$) raised the Flux
band from $\sim\SI{1}{\percent}$ to \SI{4.99}{\percent} and brought the standalone
combined budget to \SI{6.87}{\percent}. The \SI{1.4}{\percent} target-nucleon
normalisation is a fully-correlated rank-1 band.

\paragraph{$\chi^{2}$ caveat.}
The combined paper$+$ours $\chi^{2}/\mathrm{ndf}=1.481$ double-counts systematics
shared with the paper (flux/GENIE/MINOS); the ours-only inverse-covariance
$\chi^{2}$ is evaluated only with a truncated-spectral convention on the
well-constrained subspace. We therefore quote per-bin pulls, the regularisation
scan, and the truncated result together rather than treating an unconstrained
pseudo-inverse as a standalone goodness-of-fit (\S\ref{sec:openquestions}).
