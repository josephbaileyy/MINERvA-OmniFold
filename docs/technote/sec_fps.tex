\section{First full-phase-space results}
\label{sec:fps}

\textbf{Maturity:} complete measurement --- central value, full systematic
(universe/bootstrap/ML) covariance with the unified-throw adoption, a
three-prior dependence envelope, and the extension region validated by a
hidden-variable closure and 200 coverage toys.

The published phase space ($\theta_\mu<20^{\circ}$, $1.5<\pPar<\SI{60}{GeV/c}$,
$\pT<\SI{4.5}{GeV/c}$) hides a third of the signal: weighting the true
$\nu_\mu$~CC rate in the tracker fiducial volume, \SI{66.4}{\percent} lies
inside the cuts, \SI{22.3}{\percent} at $\pPar<\SI{1.5}{GeV/c}$,
\SI{11.3}{\percent} at $\theta_\mu>20^{\circ}$, and the high-$\pT$/$\pPar$
tails are negligible (Fig.~\ref{fig:fpsacc}). Because OmniFold's miss
treatment performs acceptance correction natively, removing the truth-level
kinematic cuts is a configurational change (an event-loop switch enlarging
the truth denominator from 32.8M to 49M events; the reconstruction-level
selection, including the MINOS match, is untouched), and the unfold
extrapolates into the unmeasured regions through the step-1 miss regressor.
The honest question is not \emph{whether} it can extrapolate but \emph{at
what prior cost}; this section quantifies that.

\paragraph{Validation battery.}
Three results establish the extension (Fig.~\ref{fig:fpscmp}):
\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Anchor.} Restricted to the published-phase-space sub-block,
        the full-phase-space unfold reproduces a control unfold (standard
        omnifile, paper grid, identical settings) to $0.9994$ in integral,
        with a median per-cell ratio of $1.0013$ and median deviation
        \SI{\fpsAnchor}{\percent} over all 185 reported cells --- opening the
        phase space does not damage the measurement where it was already
        made. The control itself reproduces the frozen production total
        ($\SI{\sigTwoD}{cm^2/nucleon}$) exactly.
  \item \textbf{Closure.} A self-closure on the extended grid (simulated
        reconstruction as pseudo-data) returns recovered/truth $=1.0000$ in
        every cell including the extension --- no bookkeeping or
        normalisation bias in the enlarged-denominator configuration. A
        \emph{hidden-variable} closure goes further: injecting a Gaussian
        truth bump in true $E_{\mathrm{avail}}$ (an axis the
        $(\pT,\pPar)$ unfold is blind to) and unfolding, the recovered
        $(\pT,\pPar)$ cross section reproduces the bump-reweighted truth to
        median \SI{0.17}{\percent} (90th percentile \SI{0.65}{\percent}) in
        the published cells and median \SI{0.77}{\percent} (max
        \SI{4.05}{\percent}) in the extension --- both well inside the
        tier-2 prior band.
  \item \textbf{Three-prior envelope.} Repeating the unfold with three
        priors --- MnvTune~v1, bare GENIE, and a NuWro-shaped prior (built
        by reweighting the truth $(\pT,\pPar)$ shape to two million raw
        NuWro events, which carry no phase-space cut) --- spans the total
        cross section by only $\pm\SI{1.5}{\percent}$. Per cell, the
        half-spread/mean is median \SI{2.9}{\percent} inside the published
        region but median \SI{7.9}{\percent} (90th percentile
        \SI{62}{\percent}) in the extension, concentrated in the
        zero-acceptance cells (Fig.~\ref{fig:fpsenv}).
  \item \textbf{Coverage.} 200 closure$+$bootstrap toys give per-bin
        coverage (fraction of toys with $|r|\le1$) of mean
        \SI{68.9}{\percent} against the \SI{68.27}{\percent} target
        ($\langle|r|\rangle=0.79$, target $0.80$); the published
        (\SI{68.5}{\percent}) and extension (\SI{70.0}{\percent}, marginally
        conservative) regions are both correctly calibrated --- the bootstrap
        band means what it says in the extended grid.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Result and reporting convention.}
The full-phase-space CC-inclusive cross section on the tracker fiducial
volume is
\begin{equation*}
  \sigma_{\mathrm{FPS}} = \SI{\fpsSigma}{cm^2/nucleon}
  \quad\text{(central value)},
\end{equation*}
\SI{\fpsAbove}{\percent} above the restricted-phase-space
$\SI{\sigTwoD}{cm^2/nucleon}$. We adopt \textbf{two-tier reporting}:
\emph{tier~1} (the measurement) covers cells with $\epsilon\gtrsim2\%$ ---
the published region plus the measurable extension ($\sim\SI{6}{\percent}$
of the rate, chiefly the $\theta>20^{\circ}$ wedge and the
$\pPar\in[0.75,1.5]\,\si{GeV/c}$ edge); \emph{tier~2} (prior-extrapolated)
covers the dead cells ($\sim\SI{28}{\percent}$ of the rate), published but
flagged, carrying the three-prior envelope as an explicit additional band.

The systematic campaign is complete. The full universe/bootstrap/ML budget on
the extended grid gives a block-sum combined covariance of median
\SI{7.33}{\percent} per bin (Flux-led per bin; the trace is dominated by the
\texttt{Muon\_Energy} scale, which moves the large low-$\pPar$ extension cross
section --- an FPS-specific feature). The unified-throw construction
(\S\ref{sec:uq-combine}) is \emph{mandatory} here because the migration-heavy
corner that broke the 4D block sum by $\times2$ sits inside this measurement;
on the validated miss-pinned bank (160 joint throws) it inflates the trace by
only $\times1.30$ (jitter-corrected $\times1.295$, far below the 4D
$\times2.01$), with the cross-term \SI{83}{\percent} of the block sum.
Adopting the 4D-style per-bin $\max(\sigma_{\mathrm{uni}},
\sigma_{\mathrm{block}})$ inflation onto the vertical block
(\SI{39.5}{\percent} of bins inflated, maximum factor $5.9$) gives the final
covariance, median \textbf{\SI{8.19}{\percent}} per bin, positive-definite by
construction.
