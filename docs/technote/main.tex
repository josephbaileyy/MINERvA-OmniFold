\begin{abstract}
We measure the MINERvA medium-energy FHC charged-current inclusive
double-differential cross section in muon transverse and longitudinal
momentum, $d^{2}\sigma/(d\pT\,d\pPar)$, using \emph{unbinned} OmniFold in
place of the D'Agostini iterative Bayesian unfolding (IBU) of the published
analysis (arXiv:2106.16210). On the 205 reported bins our result agrees with
the published data in normalisation ($\sigma_{\mathrm{tot}}$ ratio
$1.011$, median bin ratio $1.006$) and reproduces the published spectrum
(\SI{77.6}{\percent}/\SI{94.1}{\percent} of bins within \SI{5}{\percent}/\SI{10}{\percent}).
Our standalone uncertainty budget (median relative $\sigma$ \SI{6.87}{\percent})
matches the published total (\SI{6.86}{\percent}). We characterise the residual
$\chi^{2}/\mathrm{ndf}=3.66$ (paper covariance) as a correlated \emph{shape}
difference in the low-$\pT$ peak plus a $\sim1$-unit GBDT-estimator
regularisation band, and we compare both measurements to the GENIE MINERvA
Tune v1 prediction, which under-predicts the data by $\sim\SI{9}{\percent}$.
This note documents the method, the analysis pipeline, the uncertainty
quantification, the validation suite, and the open questions we are taking to
the collaboration.
\end{abstract}
