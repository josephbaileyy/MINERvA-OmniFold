\begin{abstract}
This is a short, jargon-light guide to a measurement of how often
muon-neutrinos interact in the MINERvA detector, and to the machine-learning
method (``unbinned OmniFold'') used to make it. It explains what the method
does in everyday terms, why it lets us measure several quantities at once where
older methods could only handle one or two, and what we found --- including a
faithful reproduction of a published result, a known nuclear-physics effect
recovered by a completely independent route, and a first measurement that no
longer throws away a third of the data. The companion analysis note carries the
full technical record; every number here is taken from it.
\end{abstract}
