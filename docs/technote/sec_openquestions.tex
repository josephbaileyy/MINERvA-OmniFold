\section{Resolution of the open questions}
\label{sec:openquestions}

The earlier draft listed six items for collaboration input. The 2026-06-03 audit
(scripts under \texttt{2d-unfolding/uq/} and \texttt{3d-unfolding/genie/}; full
log in \texttt{LITERATURE\_NOTES.md}) closes or reduces all of them. Each item
below states its resolution and what, if anything, still needs a collaborator.
Technical detail is in \texttt{uq\_statistical\_methods.tex \S sec:rank}.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Flux\,$\leftrightarrow$\,Muon\_Energy joint block and the rank
        gap --- \emph{resolved}.} The premise of the original question (``the
        paper sums more bands than we propagate'') was wrong. Our systematic-only
        covariance is rank $140/205$; the paper's
        ($\texttt{TotalCov}-\texttt{StatOnly}$) is rank $\mathbf{120}$ ---
        \emph{lower} --- so we propagate at least as many independent systematic
        directions, using the same \texttt{GetStandardSystematics}. The paper's
        $V$ is full-rank only because it includes the \emph{statistical} block; we
        match that with $\mathbf{C}^{\mathrm{syst}}+\mathbf{C}^{\mathrm{boot}}$
        (rank $201$). The one named cross-band correlation, the Bashyal
        flux\,$\leftrightarrow$\,muon-energy-scale block~\cite{Bashyal:2021tqu},
        \emph{is} rederivable and now built
        (\texttt{uq/rederive\_flux\_muonE\_cross.py}\,$\to$\,\texttt{uq/flux\_muonE\_cross.root}):
        the muon-energy-scale systematic is rank-1, so the cross term is rank-2,
        with $\rho=0.900$ (two independent estimators, $0.87$--$0.90$). It is
        physically real but adding it leaves the rank unchanged ($140\to140$) ---
        its directions already lie in $\mathrm{range}(\mathbf{C}^{\mathrm{syst}})$.
        The residual stat-block difference (our bootstrap $2.5\times$ smaller by
        trace) is explained by the data/MC split bootstrap: it is present in the
        \emph{data-only} stream ($\mathbf{C}_{\mathrm{data}}/\texttt{StatOnly}=0.356$),
        i.e.\ genuine unbinned-OmniFold statistical efficiency, not a missing MC
        term. \emph{Remaining (optional):} the fitted
        $(\Phi_{\mathrm{norm}},\Phi_{\mathrm{shape}},\mathrm{MuonE}_{\mathrm{scale}})$
        posterior from MAT-MINERvA would give a fully paper-independent $\rho$.

  \item \textbf{FrInel\_pi exclusion --- \emph{resolved (negligible)}.} An
        independent \texttt{grwght1p} pass on the 2M CV events shows both pion-FSI
        dials are sub-percent on $d\sigma/d\Eavail$: \texttt{FrInel\_pi}
        $\le\SI{0.74}{\percent}$ and \texttt{FrAbs\_pi} $\le\SI{0.82}{\percent}$
        (\S\ref{sec:3d-2p2h}). A $<\SI{1}{\percent}$ knob cannot bridge the
        $7$--$\SI{15}{\percent}$ low-$\Eavail$ excess, and we are consistent with
        MAT in excluding it. \emph{Remaining (immaterial):} a one-line collaborator
        confirmation that the MAT exclusion is still current guidance.

  \item \textbf{PPFX effective rank --- \emph{resolved}.} The $100$-universe flux
        band covariance has hard rank $99$ but a participation-ratio effective rank
        of $\approx1$: a single coherent normalization mode carries
        \SI{99.6}{\percent} of the variance ($9$ modes for \SI{99.9}{\percent}).
        So the PPFX flux uncertainty is, to excellent approximation, a fixed-rank
        (rank-1, at most rank-9) object; treating the $100$ universes as $100$
        independent directions over-counts the flux dimensionality. This is also
        why the leading flux mode correlates $\rho\approx0.9$ with the rank-1
        muon-energy-scale mode (item~1). A PCA / fixed-rank flux covariance is
        therefore both justified and recommended.

  \item \textbf{Ours-only $\chi^{2}$ --- \emph{method resolved; precedent open}.}
        The obstruction is statistical, not rank: $\mathbf{C}^{\mathrm{ours}}=
        \mathbf{C}^{\mathrm{syst}}+\mathbf{C}^{\mathrm{boot}}+\mathbf{C}^{\mathrm{ML}}$
        is rank $201$ and invertible; we report the ours-only goodness-of-fit on it
        via a truncated-spectral $\chi^{2}$ (retain
        $\lambda>10^{-10}\lambda_{\max}$), the same convention as the 3D comparison,
        and lead with per-bin pulls plus the regularization scan
        (\S\ref{sec:results-tension}). \emph{Remaining:} published precedent from
        other MAT-based OmniFold/unbinned analyses for the ours-only $\chi^{2}$
        convention.

  \item \textbf{3D systematic covariance --- \emph{method resolved; precedent
        open}.} The available-energy extension carries a full combined covariance
        (universe$+$bootstrap$+$ML) over all $1431$ reported bins, rank $247$
        (\S\ref{sec:3d-syst}); the goodness-of-fit is the rank-$247$
        truncated-spectral $\chi^{2}$ (\S\ref{sec:3d-models}), the recommended
        treatment for a rank-deficient unbinned covariance. \emph{Remaining:}
        whether there is MINERvA precedent for \emph{publishing} a 3D systematic
        covariance from low-recoil samples, and endorsement of this GoF.

  \item \textbf{High-$\Eavail$ DIS-tail excess --- \emph{localized in $W$,
        generator- and tune-robust; modelling open}.} Enabling Valencia 2p2h resolves the
        low-recoil excess but leaves a separate $+2.2\sigma$ data excess in the
        $[1.50,3.00)\,\si{GeV}$ bin (\S\ref{sec:3d-2p2h}), where 2p2h does not
        contribute and the (sub-percent) FSI dials of item~2 cannot reach. To test
        the deep-inelastic hypothesis directly we added the hadronic invariant mass
        $W$ as a validated fifth OmniFold axis (its $W$-marginal recovers the frozen
        4D cross section to \SI{0.11}{\percent}) and compared the unfolded
        $(\Eavail,W)$ cross section to the GENIE central value. The excess is
        \emph{predominantly deep-inelastic}: the two highest-$\Eavail$ bands carry
        \SI{57}{\percent} of the positive excess; of all positive excess,
        high-$\Eavail$ ($\ge\SI{0.8}{GeV}$) holds \SI{67}{\percent} and
        \SI{83}{\percent} of \emph{that} sits at $W\ge\SI{1.8}{GeV}$, with the single
        largest cell the deep-DIS corner ($\Eavail>\SI{3}{GeV}$, $W>\SI{3}{GeV}$) at
        \SI{22}{\percent}. A secondary low-$W$ ($<\SI{1.1}{GeV}$, QE-like) excess and
        a small $\Delta$-region ($W\simeq1.4$--$\SI{1.8}{GeV}$) deficit are also
        present. So the missing strength behaves like a deep-inelastic-tail
        \emph{modelling} deficit, exactly the hypothesis the $W$ axis was added to
        test. We confirm this against a \emph{generator band}: independent
        spline-normalised $(\Eavail,W)$ predictions from GENIE~CV,
        GENIE\,$+$\,Valencia~2p2h/MEC, and NuWro all underpredict the
        high-$\Eavail\times$high-$W$ corner by \SIrange{54}{58}{\percent}
        (data/generator $=1.54,\,1.58,\,1.56$). Crucially, enabling Valencia 2p2h
        does \emph{not} close it --- it slightly \emph{worsens} the corner
        ($1.54\to1.58$), since 2p2h populates low $W$ --- and the independent NuWro
        generator misses it by the same margin; at $W\in[2.2,3.0)\,\si{GeV}$ all
        three sit \SIrange{23}{25}{\percent} below the data. The DIS-tail excess is
        therefore \emph{generator- and tune-robust}: no current model reproduces it.
        \emph{Remaining:} a full $(\Eavail,W)$ covariance to attach a significance to
        the band, and the cross-check against other MINERvA inclusive low-recoil
        samples~\cite{MINERvA:2022incl}.
\end{enumerate}
