\section{Open questions for the collaboration}
\label{sec:openquestions}

These are the outstanding items where collaboration input would move the
analysis toward a defensible ours-only covariance and a published higher-D
result. Full technical context is in \texttt{uq\_statistical\_methods.tex
\S sec:rank}.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Bashyal flux\,$\leftrightarrow$\,Muon\_Energy joint block.}
        The published covariance is full rank where our per-band sum is
        $140/205$; the gap is bands we do not yet propagate, foremost the
        fitted $(\Phi_{\mathrm{norm}},\Phi_{\mathrm{shape}},\mathrm{MuonE}_{\mathrm{scale}})$
        correlation~\cite{Bashyal:2021tqu} --- the only named
        cross-band correlation, and two of our largest bands. \emph{The tension
        anatomy (\S\ref{sec:results-tension}) localises the $\chi^{2}=3.66$ to
        exactly the region these bands govern.} Is the fitted posterior
        covariance available from MAT-MINERvA, or must it be re-derived?
  \item \textbf{FrInel\_pi.} Commented out in
        \texttt{MAT-MINERvA/universes/GenieSystematics.cxx:37--38} (``should not
        currently be evaluated''). We are consistent with MAT in excluding it,
        and an independent \texttt{grwght1p} pass confirms both it
        ($\le\SI{0.74}{\percent}$ on $d\sigma/d\Eavail$) and \texttt{FrAbs\_pi}
        ($\le\SI{0.82}{\percent}$) are sub-percent (\S\ref{sec:3d-2p2h}); is the
        exclusion still current guidance?
  \item \textbf{PPFX effective rank.} Our 100 PPFX universes appear to sample a
        much lower-dimensional nuisance model. Is there a recommended
        principal-component / fixed-rank flux covariance?
  \item \textbf{Ours-only $\chi^{2}$ precedent.} Have other MAT-based
        OmniFold/unbinned analyses hit this rank issue, and what was published?
  \item \textbf{3D systematic covariance.} The available-energy extension now
        carries a full combined covariance (universe$+$bootstrap$+$ML) over all
        1431 bins (\S\ref{sec:3d-syst}); the open question is no longer how to
        build it but whether there is MINERvA precedent for \emph{publishing} a
        3D systematic covariance from low-recoil samples, and whether the
        rank-247 truncated-spectral $\chi^{2}$ (\S\ref{sec:3d-models}) is the
        recommended goodness-of-fit for a rank-deficient unbinned covariance.
  \item \textbf{High-$\Eavail$ DIS-tail excess.} Enabling Valencia 2p2h resolves
        the low-recoil excess but leaves a separate $+2.2\sigma$ data excess in
        the $[1.50,3.00)\,\si{GeV}$ bin (\S\ref{sec:3d-2p2h}), where 2p2h does
        not contribute --- a deep-inelastic-tail modelling question. Is this seen
        in other MINERvA inclusive low-recoil samples, and what tune addresses
        it?
\end{enumerate}
