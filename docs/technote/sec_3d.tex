\section{Higher-dimensional extension: available energy}
\label{sec:3d}

The enabling property of unbinned OmniFold is that adding an observable is
adding an input feature: the step-1/step-2 classifiers simply gain a column,
with no migration-matrix or binning penalty. There is no practical D'Agostini
IBU analogue, which makes this the primary physics motivation for the technique.
We exercise it by extending the measurement to
$d^{3}\sigma/(d\pT\,d\pPar\,d\Eavail)$ with available energy as a third axis.

The significance is clear from the measurement record: every published MINERvA
differential cross section has unfolded at most two kinematic variables
simultaneously (Fig.~\ref{fig:landscape}, Table~\ref{tab:landscape}), the
double-differential measurements all relying on binned IBU. A simultaneous
\emph{three}-observable unfold of MINERvA data is, to our knowledge,
without precedent, and is a direct consequence of the unbinned formulation.

\subsection{Definitions and pipeline}
\label{sec:3d-method}
Available energy follows the MINERvA low-recoil convention
(Ref.~\cite{MINERvA:2023avz}, Eq.~4): truth
$\Eavail=\sum_{p}\mathrm{KE}+\sum_{\pi^{\pm}}\mathrm{KE}+\sum_{\pi^{0},\gamma}E$
(excluding neutrons and the muon), via \texttt{CVUniverse::GetEAvailableTrue()};
reco $\Eavail$ is the calorimetric tracker$+$ECAL estimate scaled by $1.17$,
\texttt{CVUniverse::NewEavail()}. Both accessors were added to the event loop,
which was re-run over all twelve ME~FHC playlists to append the $\Eavail$
branches alongside the existing $\pT$/$\pPar$ branches. The unfolding driver is
the 2D pipeline with a three-column feature stack
$(\pT,\pPar,\Eavail)$; the cross-section extraction generalises to a 3D Jacobian
$\Delta\pT\,\Delta\pPar\,\Delta\Eavail$ with the flux $\Phi$ still applied per
$\pT$ (broadcast over $\pPar$ and $\Eavail$). The $\Eavail$ binning
$[0,0.1,0.2,0.4,0.8,1.5,3.0,100]\,\si{GeV}$ ends in a wide catch bin so that the
$\Eavail$-marginal captures the full CC-inclusive recoil tail; the global
OmniFold completeness is $c=1.0000$, as in 2D.

\subsection{The available-energy spectrum}
\label{sec:3d-spectrum}
Figure~\ref{fig:eavail} shows the unfolded $d\sigma/d\Eavail$ (the 1D projection
of the 3D result). It falls monotonically from
\SI{2.19e-38}{cm^2/GeV/nucleon} at the low-recoil peak to
\SI{6.8e-41}{} in the catch bin --- the expected CC-inclusive shape, with most
of the rate at $\Eavail\lesssim\SI{0.8}{GeV}$.

\subsection{Validation against the 2D measurement}
\label{sec:3d-anchor}
With no published 3D reference, the validation anchor is that marginalising the
3D cross section over $\Eavail$ must reproduce the 2D result of
\S\ref{sec:results}. The marginal recovers the 2D \emph{normalisation} closely:
total $\sigma$ agrees to $+\SI{0.95}{\percent}$ and the per-bin
marginal/2D ratio has median $1.0016$. The \emph{shape} agreement is looser ---
the marginal versus the published covariance gives $\chi^{2}/\mathrm{ndf}=4.98$,
above the frozen 2D values ($3.66$ default, $2.65$ LightGBM-CV), driven by a
$\sim\SI{4.4}{\percent}$ per-bin scatter that the third axis injects into the
$(\pT,\pPar)$ marginal (Fig.~\ref{fig:eavail-marg}).

\subsection{Closure: the scatter is physical, not a method bias}
\label{sec:3d-closure}
A reweight closure isolates whether that $\SI{4.4}{\percent}$ scatter is method
bias or real information. We inject a known $+\SI{30}{\percent}$ Gaussian bump
in \emph{truth} $\Eavail$ (centre \SI{0.3}{GeV}, width \SI{0.15}{GeV}) into the
pseudo-data and require the 3D unfold to recover it. It does: the recovered
step-2 mean weight is $1.048$ against the injected $1.0485$, and the residual of
the unfold against the reweighted-truth reference is, on the $\Eavail$-marginal,
median $0.9999$ with standard deviation \textbf{\num{0.0006}}
(\SI{0.06}{\percent}); the per-3D-bin residual standard deviation is
\SI{3.2}{\percent} (MC statistics in sparse high-recoil corners). The closure
marginal scatter is thus $\sim70\times$ tighter than the
$\SI{4.4}{\percent}$ data-versus-2D scatter. The method is therefore unbiased on
the new axis, and we read the elevated marginal $\chi^{2}$ as
\emph{genuine data\,$\leftrightarrow$\,MC structure that the available-energy
dimension exposes} --- information that is invisible to the 2D measurement ---
rather than a pipeline artefact (the total normalisation matches to
$<\SI{1}{\percent}$).

This establishes the 3D framework and its first closure.

\subsection{Generator comparison in 3D}
\label{sec:3d-models}
Because the published ancillary ships only binned 2D model histograms, a
quantitative comparison of the 3D result (or of arbitrary projections) requires
generating truth-level events ourselves. No detector simulation is needed --- the
unfolded result already lives at truth level --- so a generator prediction is
obtained by histogramming its truth $d^{3}\sigma$ in the same fiducial phase
space and the same truth $\Eavail$ definition. We compare four predictions:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{GENIE 2.12.10 central value} --- generated with \texttt{gevgen}
        on a CH target with the MINERvA ME~FHC flux and the
        DefaultPlusValenciaMEC configuration~\cite{Andreopoulos:2009rq}.
  \item \textbf{MINERvA Tune v1} --- the analysis tune (base GENIE $+$ RPA,
        low-recoil 2p2h, and non-resonant-pion reweights), extracted directly
        from the event-loop MC truth (the \texttt{w\_truth} weights), which
        reproduces the shipped ancillary curve to \SI{0.01}{\percent} in
        normalisation.
  \item \textbf{NuWro 21.09}~\cite{Golan:2012wx} --- an independent generator
        (C target), the same flux and phase space.
  \item \textbf{GiBUU 2019}~\cite{Buss:2011mx} --- a transport-theory generator
        (C target), built and run \emph{natively} on the compute system (no
        container), same flux and phase space.
\end{itemize}
NEUT~\cite{Hayato:2009zz} is not included (no accessible build at the time of
writing). Each prediction is normalised by its own flux-averaged total CC cross
section per nucleon ($\langle\sigma\rangle\approx\SI{3.6}{}$--$\SI{4.0e-38}{cm^2}$).

Figure~\ref{fig:3dmodels} overlays the four on the unfolded result, with the
combined-covariance systematic band of \S\ref{sec:3d-syst} on the data. The
flux-averaged totals in the measurement phase space order as
GiBUU ($\SI{2.22e-38}{}$) $<$ NuWro ($\SI{2.34e-38}{}$) $<$ GENIE CV
($\SI{2.52e-38}{}$) $<$ Tune v1 ($\SI{2.71e-38}{}$) $<$ data
($\SI{3.08e-38}{cm^2/nucleon}$): all four models under-predict the rate, by
\SIrange{12}{28}{\percent}. On $(\pT,\pPar)$ the four track the data shape
closely; the discriminating axis is $\Eavail$, where NuWro, Tune v1 and GiBUU
\emph{suppress} the low-recoil quasielastic peak below bare GENIE (RPA and
nuclear effects) --- yet the data sit \emph{above} all four in the
lowest-$\Eavail$ bins. This low-available-energy excess is invisible to the 2D
measurement and is a direct statement of the unbinned method's high-dimensional
reach.

Quantitatively, against the combined 3D covariance (\S\ref{sec:3d-syst}) the
integrated $\Eavail$ rate (catch bin dropped) places the models at
$-\SI{7.2}{\percent}$ (GENIE CV, $1.3\sigma$), $-\SI{9.5}{\percent}$ (Tune v1,
$1.8\sigma$), $-\SI{15.3}{\percent}$ (NuWro, $2.9\sigma$) and
$-\SI{21.9}{\percent}$ (GiBUU, $4.1\sigma$) below the data. A full-3D
goodness-of-fit over all 1431 bins, evaluated with a truncated-spectral
$\chi^{2}$ on the rank-247 constrained subspace of the combined covariance (a
raw pseudo-inverse of the rank-deficient matrix is never taken), excludes all
four at $p\approx0$ but ranks them sharply: diagonal-only $\chi^{2}/\mathrm{ndf}$
is Tune~v1 \textbf{4.8}, GiBUU 32.4, GENIE~CV 34, NuWro 36; the full
correlated metric is Tune~v1 131, NuWro 1287, GENIE~CV 1512, with GiBUU worst of
all because \SI{23.5}{\percent} of its residual lies \emph{outside} the
constrained subspace (its 3D shape differs in directions the covariance barely
constrains) on top of the largest normalisation offset.

\subsection{The 3D systematic-uncertainty campaign}
\label{sec:3d-syst}
The band on Fig.~\ref{fig:3dmodels} and the $\chi^{2}$ above rest on a full 3D
analogue of the 2D uncertainty budget of \S\ref{sec:systematics}. We re-ran the
event loop with the systematic universes dumped on the three-axis
$(\pT,\pPar,\Eavail)$ grid (a 187-universe sweep), propagated the bootstrap
statistical and ML-seedscan covariances, and summed them into a single combined
covariance $C_{\mathrm{syst}}+C_{\mathrm{stat}}+C_{\mathrm{ML}}$ over all 1431
reported bins. The result has $\sqrt{\operatorname{tr}}=\SI{5.72e-39}{}$, a
median per-bin relative uncertainty of \SI{10.4}{\percent}, and numerical rank
$247$ (of 1431); it is flux-dominated, with the same systematic band ordering as
the 2D measurement. This combined covariance is what projects onto each axis for
the per-bin bands and onto the full space for the goodness-of-fit, and it is the
internally-correct reference for the ``ours-only'' $\chi^{2}$ given that our
bootstrap statistical block is genuinely smaller than the paper's (an OmniFold
efficiency, confirmed by the data/MC split; see \S\ref{sec:openquestions}).

\subsection{The low-available-energy excess is a missing-2p2h signature}
\label{sec:3d-2p2h}
The low-$\Eavail$ excess is the most physically interesting feature the third
axis exposes, and the 3D framework lets us identify its origin directly by
generating the relevant physics ourselves rather than only reweighting.

\emph{Final-state interactions do not explain it.} A \texttt{grwght1p} reweight
pass on the 2M central-value GENIE events varying the pion-FSI dials shows both
are sub-percent on $d\sigma/d\Eavail$: \texttt{FrInel\_pi} shifts the spectrum by
$\le\SI{0.74}{\percent}$ (peak in $0.10$--$\SI{0.20}{GeV}$) and \texttt{FrAbs\_pi}
by $\le\SI{0.82}{\percent}$ (peak in the lowest bin), with the integrated rate
moving only $\pm\SI{0.02}{}$--$\SI{0.03}{\percent}$ at $\pm1\sigma$. A
$<\SI{1}{\percent}$ knob cannot bridge a $7$--$\SI{15}{\percent}$ excess, so the
excess points to the initial-state/nuclear model, not rescattering.

\emph{The shape and size match 2p2h.} A subtlety drives the diagnosis: our base
GENIE central value was generated with the default event-generator list, which
contains \emph{no} meson-exchange-current (2p2h/MEC) events
($\texttt{mec}=0$ for all $1.48\times10^{6}$ CC events; the modes are QE
\SI{11}{\percent}, RES \SI{22}{\percent}, DIS \SI{66}{\percent}, COH
\SI{0.9}{\percent}). Any 2p2h is therefore \emph{added}, not reweighted.
Decomposing the central-value $d\sigma/d\Eavail$ by interaction mode (per-bin
mode count-fractions $\times$ the bin value) and overlaying the data localises
the excess to the quasielastic--$\Delta$ dip: the $[0.10,0.20)$ bin is
$+3.9\sigma$ and $[0.20,0.40)$ is $+2.3\sigma$, while the QE-dominated
$[0,0.10)$ already matches ($+0.8\sigma$) and $[0.40,1.50)$ match to
$\pm0.4\sigma$. \SI{57}{\percent} of the integrated deficit sits at
$\Eavail\le\SI{0.4}{GeV}$, exactly where 2p2h lives, and closing it requires a
2p2h component $\approx\SI{43}{\percent}$ of the QE rate
(\SI{62}{\percent} locally) --- the standard empirical/Valencia-MEC magnitude,
in contrast to the sub-percent FSI dials (Fig.~\ref{fig:modedecomp}).

\emph{Enabling Valencia 2p2h confirms it.} We regenerated the GENIE central
value with the empirical 2p2h turned on (\texttt{--event-generator-list
Default+CCMEC}, 2M events). Because the Nieves--Simo--Vacas MEC channel, though
present in the splines, is absent from the \texttt{gspl2root} total-CC graph, the
sample is normalised by the non-MEC CC count (anchoring QE$+$RES$+$DIS$+$COH to
the known total and letting MEC add on top by $1/(1-f_{\mathrm{MEC}})$, with
$f_{\mathrm{MEC}}=\SI{2.87}{\percent}$). The added 2p2h lands squarely in the dip
and moves GENIE toward the data: the $[0,0.10)$ bin goes from $+0.8\sigma$ to
$-0.04\sigma$ (onto the data), $[0.10,0.20)$ from $+3.9\sigma$ to $+2.85\sigma$,
and $[0.20,0.40)$ from $+2.3\sigma$ to $+1.0\sigma$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:mec}). It
fills \SI{46}{\percent} of the data$-$CV gap in the dip and \SI{27}{\percent} of
the integrated deficit (CV $-\SI{7.2}{\percent}\to-\SI{5.2}{\percent}$), and
improves the full-3D $\chi^{2}/\mathrm{ndf}$ from $1512$ to $1145$
($-\SI{24}{\percent}$). It remains well short of Tune v1 ($131$), because Tune v1
layers MINERvA's empirical low-recoil 2p2h \emph{enhancement} and RPA on top of
the stock Valencia MEC --- i.e.\ stock Valencia 2p2h is real but under-strength
for MINERvA low recoil, which is precisely the known MINERvA-tune story and is
consistent with NuWro and GiBUU (native, un-enhanced 2p2h) sitting low as well.
The one feature 2p2h does not touch is a separate $+2.2\sigma$ excess in the
high-$\Eavail$ $[1.50,3.00)$ bin, a DIS-tail modelling issue
(\S\ref{sec:openquestions}).

This low-available-energy deficit is the same feature MINERvA's dedicated low-recoil
inclusive measurement reports: Ascencio \emph{et al.}~\cite{MINERvA:2022incl}, which
measures $d^{2}\sigma/(dq_{3}\,d\Eavail)$ for $q_{3}<\SI{1.2}{GeV}$ at the same
$\langle E_\nu\rangle\sim\SI{6}{GeV}$, finds GENIE and NuWro underpredicting the data in
the low-$\Eavail$ region absent an enhanced 2p2h component. Our excess appears in the
muon-kinematics extension (integrated over all $q_3$) rather than at fixed $q_3$, so the
comparison is of the $\Eavail$ \emph{shape} and direction rather than absolute
normalisation; the consistency confirms that the OmniFold 3D extension recovers the
established MINERvA low-recoil/2p2h feature as a by-product of the muon-kinematics
unfolding. The overlay tooling
(\texttt{3d-unfolding/genie/compare\_ascencio\_eavail.py}) is in place for a quantitative
shape comparison once the \cite{MINERvA:2022incl} data release is loaded.
