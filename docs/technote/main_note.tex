\title{Unbinned OmniFold measurement of the MINERvA ME FHC\\
CC-inclusive double-differential cross section\\
$d^{2}\sigma/(d\pT\,d\pPar)$ and its higher-dimensional extensions\\[4pt]
\large MINERvA analysis note --- DRAFT}
\author{J.~R.~Bailey}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We apply \emph{unbinned} OmniFold to the MINERvA medium-energy FHC
charged-current inclusive cross section, replacing the iterative Bayesian
unfolding of the published analysis (arXiv:2106.16210). In two dimensions,
$d^{2}\sigma/(d\pT\,d\pPar)$, our result reproduces the published spectrum
($\sigma_{\mathrm{tot}}$ ratio $\ratioTot$; \SI{\binsTen}{\percent} of bins within
\SI{10}{\percent}) with a matching uncertainty budget (median relative $\sigma$
\SI{\uqMedian}{\percent} vs \SI{\uqPaper}{\percent}).

Because adding an observable is just adding a feature---with no migration matrix
or binning penalty---we then perform the first \emph{simultaneous three- and
four-observable} unfolds of MINERvA data, adding available energy ($\Eavail$)
and three-momentum transfer ($q_{3}$). Each higher-dimensional result reproduces
the lower-dimensional one under marginalisation (a built-in closure), and four
generators we produce at truth level (GENIE, the MINERvA Tune, NuWro, GiBUU) all
under-predict the data at low $\Eavail$. This is the well-established MINERvA
low-recoil/2p2h deficit (Rodrigues 2016; Ascencio 2022): pion final-state
interactions move $d\sigma/d\Eavail$ by $<\SI{1}{\percent}$, whereas adding the
empirical Valencia 2p2h fills the quasielastic--$\Delta$ dip. Recovering this
known feature through an independent method validates the technique; the
four-dimensional $(\Eavail,q_{3})$ result additionally resolves the deficit's
momentum-transfer structure, and a bin-identical full-covariance comparison to
the published Ascencio low-recoil measurement (arXiv:2110.13372) on their
maximal common grid gives $\chi^{2}/\mathrm{ndf}=\ascChi/\ascNdf$ ($p=\ascP$) --- the
two measurements are consistent. A fifth axis, the hadronic
invariant mass $W$, localises the remaining high-$\Eavail$ excess to the
deep-inelastic region: with a dedicated 42-bin $(\Eavail,W)$ covariance, all
four generators miss the data in the high-$W$ corner
($\Eavail\ge\SI{0.4}{GeV}$, $W\ge\SI{1.8}{GeV}$) at $\disCornerLo$--$\disCornerHi\sigma$.

The 2D, 3D and 4D results carry full covariance budgets
($\mathrm{universe}+\mathrm{bootstrap}+\mathrm{ML}$); in 4D a 160-throw
\emph{unified-throw} study shows the block sum underestimates the vertical
systematic by a factor $\sim2$, and the published 4D covariance adopts the
unified-throw magnitude. The method passes binned and unbinned goodness-of-fit
tests plus scalar GBDT/MLP classifier cross-checks. A point-cloud transformer
(PET) pipeline operating on the raw reconstructed calorimetric clusters now
produces a full-statistics, absolutely-normalised cross section: its closure is
unbiased to $\sim\SI{\petClosure}{\percent}$, it agrees with the production GBDT result
at the \SI{9}{\percent} (training-configuration) level, and it carries its own
frozen-reweighter systematic budget. Finally, removing the muon kinematic cuts
from the signal definition yields a first \emph{full-phase-space} CC-inclusive
cross section on the tracker fiducial volume,
$\sigma_{\mathrm{FPS}}=\SI{\fpsSigma}{cm^2/nucleon}$ (\SI{\fpsAbove}{\percent} above
the restricted phase space, which hides a third of the rate): inside the
published region it reproduces the restricted measurement to
\SI{\fpsAnchor}{\percent} median per cell, and a three-prior envelope quantifies the
extrapolation cost (few-percent where the detector has acceptance, explicit
tens-of-percent flags in the dead regions). This note documents the experiment
and samples, the method, the analysis pipeline, the uncertainty quantification,
the validation suite, the higher-dimensional extensions, the full-phase-space
extension, and the statistical methodology (as appendices).
\end{abstract}
