\section{Summary and outlook}
\label{sec:summary}

\paragraph{Summary.}
Unbinned OmniFold with gradient-boosted trees reproduces the published
MINERvA ME FHC CC-inclusive $d^{2}\sigma/(d\pT\,d\pPar)$
($\sigma_{\mathrm{tot}}$ ratio $1.011$; \SI{94}{\percent} of bins within
\SI{10}{\percent}; pull RMS $0.60$) with a standalone uncertainty budget that
matches the published total (\SI{6.87}{\percent} vs \SI{6.86}{\percent}
median per bin) and passes a full validation suite: three closure families, a
bottom-line test, frequentist coverage, binned and unbinned (C2ST)
goodness-of-fit, and classifier-family cross-checks. On that foundation we
performed the first simultaneous three-, four- and five-observable unfolds of
MINERvA data ($+\Eavail$, $+q_{3}$, $+W$), each validated by recovering its
lower-dimensional predecessor under marginalisation. The physics output:
\begin{itemize}[itemsep=2pt]
  \item the low-$\Eavail$ excess over all four truth-level generators
        recovers the established MINERvA low-recoil/2p2h deficit through an
        independent method, with the expected FSI insensitivity and Valencia
        2p2h response (\S\ref{sec:3d-2p2h});
  \item the $(\Eavail,q_{3})$ plane resolves the deficit's momentum-transfer
        structure and shifts the systematic budget from flux- to
        model-dominated (\S\ref{sec:4d});
  \item the 4D unified-throw study found the block-sum covariance
        underestimates the vertical systematic by $\sim2\times$, and the
        published 4D covariance adopts the corrected magnitude
        (\S\ref{sec:uq-combine}) --- a methodological result in its own
        right;
  \item the $(\Eavail,W)$ study localises the remaining high-$\Eavail$
        excess to the deep-inelastic region: all four generators miss the
        high-$W$ corner at $9$--$18\sigma$ on a dedicated covariance
        (\S\ref{sec:eavailw});
  \item the PET point-cloud track yields a full-statistics,
        absolutely-normalised cross section from the raw reconstructed
        clusters, closure-validated and budgeted (\S\ref{sec:pet}).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Outlook: completing the full-phase-space measurement.}
The full-phase-space extension is no longer prospective: \S\ref{sec:fps}
reports the validated central value
($\sigma_{\mathrm{FPS}}=\SI{4.502e-38}{cm^2/nucleon}$, \SI{46}{\percent}
above the restricted phase space) with its anchor, closure, and three-prior
envelope. What remains is its uncertainty campaign --- the full
universe/bootstrap/ML budget on the extended grid with the mandatory
unified-throw construction (\S\ref{sec:uq-combine}), plus hidden-variable
closure and coverage toys exercised specifically in the extension regions
--- all in production at the time of writing. Pending items inherited from
this note: NEUT as a fifth generator, per-lateral PET re-inference, the
finer 44-cell Ascencio comparison (the maximal-common-grid version is done
and consistent, \S\ref{sec:4d}), and the remaining collaborator confirmations
of App.~\ref{app:openq}.
