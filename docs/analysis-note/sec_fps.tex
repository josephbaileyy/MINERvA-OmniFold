\section{Extended fiducial phase-space study}
\label{sec:fps}

This section reports a preliminary scalar central value, a three-prior dependence
envelope, and an extension region tested by a hidden-variable closure and
200 coverage toys.  The former full systematic covariance and unified-throw
adoption are withdrawn while the corrected uncertainty production is run;
uncertainty-derived extended-fiducial comparisons are not quoted in the interim.
The signal remains $\nu_\mu$ CC in the tracker interaction fiducial volume:
truth-muon kinematic cuts are removed, but the reconstructed MINOS-based
selection is unchanged. This is therefore an extended \emph{fiducial}
measurement domain, not unrestricted neutrino phase space.

The published phase space ($\theta_\mu<20^{\circ}$, $1.5<\pPar<\SI{60}{GeV/c}$,
$\pT<\SI{4.5}{GeV/c}$) hides a third of the signal: weighting the true
$\nu_\mu$~CC rate in the tracker fiducial volume, \SI{66.4}{\percent} lies
inside the cuts, \SI{22.3}{\percent} at $\pPar<\SI{1.5}{GeV/c}$,
\SI{11.3}{\percent} at $\theta_\mu>20^{\circ}$, and the high-$\pT$/$\pPar$
tails are negligible (Fig.~\ref{fig:fpsacc}). Because OmniFold's miss
treatment performs acceptance correction natively, removing the truth-level
kinematic cuts is a configurational change (an event-loop switch enlarging
the truth denominator from 32.8M to 49M events; the reconstruction-level
selection, including the MINOS match, is untouched), and the unfold
extrapolates into the unmeasured regions through the step-1 miss regressor.
The question is not \emph{whether} it can extrapolate but \emph{at
what prior cost}; this section quantifies that.

\paragraph{Validation battery.}
Three results establish the extension (Fig.~\ref{fig:fpscmp}):
\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Anchor.} Restricted to the published-phase-space sub-block,
        the extended-fiducial unfold reproduces a control unfold (standard
        omnifile, paper grid, identical settings) to $0.9994$ in integral,
        with a median per-cell ratio of $1.0013$ and median deviation
        \SI{\fpsAnchor}{\percent} over all 185 reported cells --- opening the
        phase space does not damage the measurement where it was already
        made. The control itself reproduces the frozen production total
        ($\SI{\sigTwoD}{cm^2/nucleon}$) exactly.
  \item \textbf{Closure.} A self-closure on the extended grid (simulated
        reconstruction as pseudo-data) returns recovered/truth $=1.0000$ in
        every cell including the extension --- no bookkeeping or
        normalization bias in the enlarged-denominator configuration. A
        \emph{hidden-variable} closure goes further: injecting a Gaussian
        truth bump in true $E_{\mathrm{avail}}$ (an axis the
        $(\pT,\pPar)$ unfold is blind to) and unfolding, the recovered
        $(\pT,\pPar)$ cross section reproduces the bump-reweighted truth to
        median \SI{0.17}{\percent} (90th percentile \SI{0.65}{\percent}) in
        the published cells and median \SI{0.77}{\percent} (max
        \SI{4.05}{\percent}) in the extension --- both well inside the
        tier-2 prior band.
  \item \textbf{Three-prior envelope.} Repeating the unfold with three
        priors --- MnvTune~v1, bare GENIE, and a NuWro-shaped prior (built
        by reweighting the truth $(\pT,\pPar)$ shape to two million raw
        NuWro events, which carry no phase-space cut) --- spans the total
        cross section by only $\pm\SI{1.5}{\percent}$. Per cell, the
        half-spread/mean is median \SI{2.9}{\percent} inside the published
        region but median \SI{7.9}{\percent} (90th percentile
        \SI{62}{\percent}) in the extension, concentrated in the
        zero-acceptance cells (Fig.~\ref{fig:fpsenv}).
  \item \textbf{Coverage.} 200 closure$+$bootstrap toys give per-bin
        coverage (fraction of toys with $|r|\le1$) of mean
        \SI{68.9}{\percent} against the \SI{68.27}{\percent} target
        ($\langle|r|\rangle=0.79$, target $0.80$); the published
        (\SI{68.5}{\percent}) and extension (\SI{70.0}{\percent}, marginally
        conservative) regions are both correctly calibrated --- the bootstrap
        band means what it says in the extended grid.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Result and reporting convention.}
The preliminary extended-fiducial CC-inclusive central value on the tracker
fiducial volume is
\begin{equation*}
  \sigma_{\mathrm{ext}} = \SI{\fpsSigma}{cm^2/nucleon}
  \quad\text{(central value)},
\end{equation*}
\SI{\fpsAbove}{\percent} above the restricted-phase-space
$\SI{\sigTwoD}{cm^2/nucleon}$. We adopt \textbf{two-tier reporting}:
\emph{tier~1} (acceptance-supported reporting) covers cells with $\epsilon\gtrsim2\%$ ---
the published region plus the measurable extension ($\sim\SI{6}{\percent}$
of the rate, chiefly the $\theta>20^{\circ}$ wedge and the
$\pPar\in[0.75,1.5]\,\si{GeV/c}$ edge); \emph{tier~2} (prior-extrapolated)
covers the dead cells ($\sim\SI{28}{\percent}$ of the rate), separately
flagged and carrying the three-prior envelope as an explicit additional band.
The total over both tiers must not be read as uniformly data-constrained.

The extended-fiducial central value, restricted-region anchor, and three-prior envelope remain
current. Its former universe/bootstrap/ML budget and unified-throw adoption used
the superseded one-sided, CV-centered, jitter-subtracted construction and are
unquotable. Corrected extended-fiducial uncertainty production must use the contract in
\S\ref{sec:uq-combine} and selection-complete lateral support appropriate to
the enlarged phase space. No replacement uncertainty magnitude is asserted
here.

\subsection{Recoil-PET feasibility study on the extended population}
\label{sec:fps-pet}
An existing PET campaign trained at full statistics on an enlarged event
population and extended reporting grid. Its classifier representation,
however, remained recoil-only: step~1 saw non-muon reconstructed clusters and
step~2 saw truth hadrons with the muon removed. The muon coordinates used to
define and bin the extension were not classifier inputs. Those products are
therefore retained only as feasibility diagnostics for the recoil estimator;
their populated-bin counts, prior-transfer envelope and retraining floor are
not a final full-event measurement and are not used to argue that
low-efficiency cells are data-constrained.

The replacement estimator must satisfy two independent gates. It must first
carry a distinguished muon and validated event context through separate
observable reco/data and truth interfaces, and pass an omitted-variable stress
closure that shifts muon kinematics at fixed recoil. It must also consume the
canonical extended-fiducial event loops and $15\times19$ muon grid while
preserving the enlarged truth denominator, native misses and unchanged
reconstructed selection. The interface implementation is in progress; no
full-event nominal or uncertainty is claimed here.

After those gates, the full-event nominal, ordinary and stress closures,
acceptance census, anchor, coverage and prior envelope will be repeated before
a fresh statistical, ML, vertical, retraining-response and
selection-complete-lateral UQ campaign. No covariance component from the
recoil-only PET estimator is transferred automatically. The reporting boundary
will be set by those validations: additional features can use more observed
information, but cannot create detector information in zero-efficiency cells.
