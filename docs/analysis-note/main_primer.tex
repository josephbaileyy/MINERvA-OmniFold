\begin{abstract}
This is a short, jargon-light guide to a measurement of how often
muon-neutrinos interact in the MINERvA detector, and to the machine-learning
method (``unbinned OmniFold'') used to make it. It explains what the method
does in everyday terms, why it lets us measure several quantities at once without
an explicitly binned response tensor that grows rapidly with dimension, and what we found --- including a
faithful reproduction of a published result, a known nuclear-physics effect
recovered by a completely independent route, and a study of how far the signal
definition can be extended beyond the published muon kinematic cuts. That
extension contains both detector-supported and simulation-dominated regions,
which are kept separate.
\end{abstract}
