\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

Throughout this section, error bars on the unfolded points are the total
uncertainty --- the combined systematic (universe), statistical (bootstrap),
and ML (seedscan) budget constructed in \S\ref{sec:systematics}; the
published points carry the published total covariance.

\subsection{Double-differential cross section}
\label{sec:results-xsec}
Figure~\ref{fig:xsec} shows the unfolded $d^{2}\sigma/(d\pT\,d\pPar)$ and its
1D projections. The total cross section is
\SI{\sigTwoD}{cm^2/nucleon} (published: \SI{\sigTwoDpaper}{cm^2/nucleon}).

The shapes are those expected for CC-inclusive scattering at
$\langle E_\nu\rangle\sim\SI{6}{GeV}$. The $\pT$ projection vanishes at
$\pT=0$ (phase space), rises to a broad maximum at
$\pT\approx0.55$--$\SI{0.70}{GeV/c}$, and falls by more than two orders of
magnitude toward the \SI{4.5}{GeV/c} edge. The peak position is set by the
momentum-transfer scale of few-GeV neutrino scattering: for the forward
muons selected here $\pT^{2}\simeq Q^{2}\,E_\mu/E_\nu$, and the cross
section concentrates at $Q^{2}\lesssim\SI{1}{GeV^2}$ --- nucleon
form-factor suppression in the quasi-elastic and resonance channels,
structure-function kinematics in the deep-inelastic contribution --- which
with $E_\mu/E_\nu\sim0.6$ gives the observed $\pT\sim\SI{0.6}{GeV/c}$
scale. The $\pPar$ projection rises from the \SI{1.5}{GeV/c} threshold to
a broad maximum at $\pPar\approx3$--$\SI{4.5}{GeV/c}$, the image of the
NuMI medium-energy flux peak at $E_\nu\approx\SI{6}{GeV}$, and falls
steadily thereafter (two orders of magnitude by
$\pPar\approx\SI{20}{GeV/c}$). In the 2D map the $\theta_\mu<20^{\circ}$
acceptance empties the high-$\pT$, low-$\pPar$ corner (the 19 unreported
diagonal cells of \S\ref{sec:method-binning}).

\subsection{Comparison to the published measurement}
\label{sec:results-compare}
Table~\ref{tab:compare} quantifies the agreement with
Ref.~\cite{MINERvA:2021owq} over the 205 reported bins. The total cross
section reproduces the published one to \SI{1.1}{\percent} (ratio
$\ratioTot$), with a median per-bin ratio of $\medianBinRatio$;
$\binsFive\%$ of bins agree within \SI{5}{\percent} and $\binsTen\%$ within
\SI{10}{\percent}. Two $\chi^{2}$ figures are quoted: against the published
covariance alone ($\chiPaper$ per degree of freedom) and against the
combined covariance that also credits this measurement's own uncertainty
($\chiCombined$); the anatomy of the former is examined in
\S\ref{sec:results-tension}. The per-bin pull distribution computed with
the published covariance (Fig.~\ref{fig:pull}) is centred (mean
$\pullMean$) with RMS $\pullRMSr$, well inside the published
uncertainties, and Figs.~\ref{fig:ptslices}--\ref{fig:pzslices} show the
same comparison bin-by-bin in $\pT$ and $\pPar$ slices.

\subsection{Anatomy of the $\chi^{2}=3.66$ tension}
\label{sec:results-tension}
The central values agree (pull RMS $\pullRMS$, so a diagonal-only $\chi^{2}/\mathrm{ndf}$
would be $0.37$), yet the full paper-covariance $\chi^{2}/\mathrm{ndf}$ is $\chiPaper$:
a $10\times$ inflation living entirely in the off-diagonal structure of the
published covariance. The eigenmode decomposition (Fig.~\ref{fig:tension}) shows
the tension is carried by moderate-$\lambda$ modes with $5$--$6\sigma$ eigen-pulls
(the 10 smallest-$\lambda$ modes carry only \SI{3}{\percent}; keeping the 73
best-measured directions still gives $1.42$), so it is \emph{not} a small-$\lambda$
inversion artifact. It is \emph{shape}, not normalization
($\chi^{2}_{\mathrm{norm}}=0.10$ vs $\chi^{2}_{\mathrm{shape}}=750$), localised to
the low-$\pT$ cross-section peak ridge where the flux and muon-energy bands
dominate. A $\sim1$-unit methodological band comes from the GBDT estimator
(exact-GBT $\chiPaper$ vs HistGBT $2.70\pm0.04$ vs LightGBM $2.65$; iteration count is
negligible, $5\to10$ iters move it by $+0.01$). Full treatment:
App.~\ref{sec:tension}. The eigenmode-localisation maps and the additional
model-comparison ratio maps are shown in
Figs.~\ref{fig:tension}--\ref{fig:tensionmodes}.

\subsection{Comparison to GENIE MINERvA Tune v1}
\label{sec:results-model}
Both the published data and our unfolded result are compared to the GENIE
2.12.6~\cite{Andreopoulos:2009rq} MINERvA Tune v1 prediction shipped with the
ancillary (Fig.~\ref{fig:model}). The tune
under-predicts the total by $\sim\SI{9}{\percent}$
($\sigma_{\mathrm{tune}}/\sigma_{\mathrm{data}}=0.91$), concentrated in the
low-$\pT$ peak, and is strongly disfavoured by the data covariance
($\chi^{2}/\mathrm{ndf}$: data vs tune $33.0$, ours vs tune $26.5$). Our unfolded
result tracks the data far more closely than the model does.
The published analysis~\cite{MINERvA:2021owq}
additionally compares to NuWro~\cite{Golan:2012wx}, GiBUU~\cite{Buss:2011mx} and
NEUT~\cite{Hayato:2009zz}; because OmniFold delivers a per-event reweighted
truth ensemble, any such generator prediction can be compared to our result in
\emph{any} projection or dimension without re-unfolding --- a capability we
exploit for the 3D extension (\S\ref{sec:3d}).
