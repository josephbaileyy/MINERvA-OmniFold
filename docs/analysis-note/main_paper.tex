\title{First unbinned simultaneous multi-observable measurement of the
MINERvA medium-energy CC-inclusive cross section}
\author{Joseph Bailey \and Benjamin Nachman}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We report the first application of unbinned machine-learning unfolding
(OmniFold) to MINERvA medium-energy $\nu_\mu$ data. The unbinned
$d^{2}\sigma/(d\pT\,d\pPar)$ reproduces the published iterative-Bayesian
measurement ($\sigma_{\mathrm{tot}}$ ratio $\ratioTot$; median per-bin
uncertainty \SI{\uqMedian}{\percent} vs \SI{\uqPaper}{\percent}). Because adding
an observable costs only an input feature, we then measure the first
simultaneous three-, four- and five-observable MINERvA cross sections, adding
available energy $\Eavail$, momentum transfer $q_{3}$ and hadronic mass $W$. The
low-$\Eavail$ region independently recovers the known low-recoil/2p2h deficit
and agrees at central-value level with the published low-recoil measurement
(the corrected covariance comparison is pending); the $(\Eavail,W)$ plane
localizes a residual deep-inelastic excess that all tested generators
underpredict, with corrected significance pending. A point-cloud
transformer reproduces the result in the full phase space of raw
calorimeter-cluster and truth-particle inputs, and removing the muon kinematic
cuts yields a first extended-phase-space cross section,
$\sigma_{\mathrm{ext}}=\SI{\fpsSigma}{cm^2/nucleon}$.
\end{abstract}
