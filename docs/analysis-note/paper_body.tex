\section{Introduction}
Differential cross sections from neutrino experiments are extracted by
\emph{unfolding} the detector response. The standard tool, iterative Bayesian
unfolding on a binned migration matrix, scales poorly: each additional observable
multiplies the bin count, which has limited every published MINERvA differential
measurement to at most two simultaneously unfolded kinematic variables. Unbinned
machine-learning unfolding (OmniFold~\cite{Andreassen:2019cjw,Andreassen:2021zzk})
removes that limit by learning per-event weights in the full observable space, so
that adding an observable costs only an input feature.

We report the first application of OmniFold to MINERvA medium-energy (ME) FHC
$\nu_\mu$ data. We first reproduce the published double-differential
CC-inclusive cross section $d^{2}\sigma/(d\pT\,d\pPar)$~\cite{MINERvA:2021owq} as
a validation, then exploit the unbinned formulation to measure the first
simultaneous three-, four- and five-observable MINERvA cross sections, adding
available energy $\Eavail$, three-momentum transfer $q_{3}$, and hadronic mass
$W$. These expose the known low-recoil/2p2h deficit through an independent route,
localize a residual deep-inelastic excess that no current generator reproduces,
and --- together with a full-phase-space point-cloud cross-check and
an extended-phase-space result --- demonstrate the reach of the method.

\section{Data and method}
The analysis uses the twelve ME FHC playlists ($1.057\times10^{21}$ protons on
target, $\langle E_\nu\rangle\sim\SI{6}{GeV}$), with the signal definition,
fiducial volume, flux~\cite{MINERvA:2016iqn} and selection of
Ref.~\cite{MINERvA:2021owq}; after selection the data sample is $4.12$M events
against $32.8$M simulated signal events (GENIE 2.12.6 with the MINERvA Tune v1).
A dedicated event loop writes per-event truth and reconstructed observables for
the unbinned unfold.

OmniFold unfolds by iterated reweighting. Each iteration trains a classifier to
separate data from reconstructed simulation, converts its output to a per-event
likelihood-ratio weight, pulls that weight back to truth through the
simulation's truth--reco pairing (treating unmatched ``miss'' events natively, so
no separate efficiency correction is applied), and trains a second classifier to
propagate it to a smooth truth-level reweight for the next iteration. We use
gradient-boosted decision trees (LightGBM, 100 trees, 5 iterations) as the
learners; the higher-dimensional unfolds simply append feature columns
$(\pT,\pPar,\Eavail,q_{3},W)$. Histogramming the reweighted truth ensemble (in
any binning, chosen after unfolding) and dividing by flux, exposure and
target-nucleon count gives the cross section. The full uncertainty budget
combines systematic (MAT universe), statistical (Poisson bootstrap) and ML
(seedscan) covariances; the complete statistical methodology, calibration and
validation suite are documented in the companion analysis note.

\section{Two-dimensional reproduction}
The unbinned $d^{2}\sigma/(d\pT\,d\pPar)$ reproduces the published measurement
(Fig.~\ref{fig:pxsec}): the total cross section is \SI{\sigTwoD}{cm^2/nucleon}
(published \SI{\sigTwoDpaper}{cm^2/nucleon}, ratio $\ratioTot$),
\SI{\binsTen}{\percent} of the 205 reported bins agree within \SI{10}{\percent},
and the per-bin pulls against the published covariance are sub-$1\sigma$ (RMS
$\pullRMS$). Our standalone uncertainty budget matches the published total in
size (median per-bin \SI{\uqMedian}{\percent} vs \SI{\uqPaper}{\percent}),
flux-dominated as in the publication. The residual full-covariance
$\chi^{2}/\mathrm{ndf}=\chiPaper$ is shape, not normalization, and traces to the
off-diagonal structure of the published covariance on the low-$\pT$ peak; it
carries a $\sim1$-unit method-dependence band from the choice of GBDT estimator.

\section{Higher-dimensional cross sections}

\subsection{Available energy: the low-recoil deficit}
Extending to $(\pT,\pPar,\Eavail)$ and then $(\pT,\pPar,\Eavail,q_{3})$ yields the
first three- and four-observable MINERvA unfolds; each reproduces its
lower-dimensional predecessor under marginalization. Four truth-level generators
(GENIE CV, MINERvA Tune v1, NuWro, GiBUU) all underpredict the data at low
$\Eavail$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:pgen}), the established MINERvA low-recoil/2p2h deficit.
We confirm its origin directly: pion final-state-interaction dials move
$d\sigma/d\Eavail$ by $<\SI{1}{\percent}$, whereas adding the empirical Valencia
2p2h fills the quasielastic--$\Delta$ dip. The four-dimensional $(\Eavail,q_{3})$
result resolves the deficit's momentum-transfer structure, and a bin-identical
comparison to the published low-recoil measurement on the maximal common
grid~\cite{MINERvA:2022incl} agrees at central-value level. The former
covariance-based consistency metric is withheld pending corrected production
(Fig.~\ref{fig:pasc}).

\subsection{Hadronic mass: a deep-inelastic excess no generator reproduces}
Adding $W$ as a fifth axis isolates the discrepancy that survives 2p2h. The
unfolded-minus-generator excess concentrates at high $\Eavail$ \emph{and} high
$W$ --- the deep-inelastic region (Fig.~\ref{fig:pdis}). All four generators
underpredict the central result in the high-$W$ corner. Exact significances are
withheld while the corrected five-dimensional statistical and systematic
covariances are regenerated. Enabling Valencia
2p2h does not close the corner --- it slightly worsens it, because 2p2h populates
low $W$ --- and the independent NuWro and GiBUU generators miss it by the same or
a larger margin. The excess is thus a genuinely deep-inelastic ($W\gtrsim\SI{1.8}{GeV}$)
modeling deficit, robust across generator and tune.

\section{Method robustness and the point-cloud track}
The unfolded result is stable across the classifier family (GBDT, MLP,
transformer) and the input representation. A point-cloud transformer
(PET~\cite{Mikuni:2024qsr}) unfolding the \emph{raw} reconstructed calorimeter
clusters reproduces the scalar-GBDT result and, trained to full statistics,
yields an absolutely-normalized cross section whose closure is unbiased to
$\sim\SI{\petClosure}{\percent}$ and which agrees with the production result at the
\SI{9}{\percent} (training-configuration) level (Fig.~\ref{fig:ppet}). This
establishes the infrastructure for unfolding directly on low-level detector data.
Corrected five-dimensional joint throws now test the standard block-sum
covariance with asymmetric endpoints, a fixed estimator seed, and
mean-centered throws. Their numerical adoption awaits the corrected
statistical/ML budget and support-migration gate; the earlier 4D adoption is
not quoted. The PET background-subtracted target and retrained statistical/ML
components pass, but no PET--GBDT precision claim is made before its corrected
systematic and lateral blocks are complete.

\section{Extended-phase-space cross section}
The published phase space discards about a third of the signal rate (events with
the muon below \SI{1.5}{GeV/c} or beyond $20^{\circ}$). Because OmniFold corrects
for acceptance natively, removing those truth-level cuts is a configuration change,
and the unfold extrapolates into the unmeasured regions through the miss
regressor. The resulting extended-phase-space CC-inclusive cross section is
$\sigma_{\mathrm{ext}}=\SI{\fpsSigma}{cm^2/nucleon}$, \SI{\fpsAbove}{\percent}
above the restricted measurement (Fig.~\ref{fig:pfps}). Inside the published
region it reproduces the restricted result to \SI{\fpsAnchor}{\percent} median per
cell; a three-prior envelope reports the extrapolation cost cell by cell
(few-percent where the detector has acceptance, explicit tens-of-percent flags in
the dead regions). Its full covariance is being regenerated under the corrected
uncertainty contract. The same extension is realized with the
point-cloud engine at full statistics on the 5D grid, with an extended
catch-bin grid, two-tier reporting by reconstruction efficiency
(\num{9307}~measured, \num{2568}~extrapolated bins), a three-prior envelope
that stays flat (16--19\% median) rather than blooming outside the measured
region, and a seed-replica retraining floor an order of magnitude below that
envelope.

\section{Conclusions}
Unbinned OmniFold reproduces a flagship MINERvA cross section and its uncertainty
budget, and enables measurements binned methods cannot: the first simultaneous
three-, four- and five-observable MINERvA unfolds. These independently recover the
known low-recoil/2p2h deficit, agree with the dedicated published low-recoil
measurement, and localize a deep-inelastic excess in a high-$W$ corner that all
tested generators underpredict; its corrected significance is pending. A
point-cloud transformer extends the program to the full phase space
of variable-length inputs and outputs, and a first extended-phase-space cross
section recovers the rate that restricted phase
spaces discard. The complete methodology, validation suite and per-number record
are in the accompanying analysis note.
