\section{Recoil-point-cloud representation cross-check}
\label{sec:pet}

% \bpn{left off here}

As a low-level-representation cross-check, a point-cloud transformer (PET, the architecture of
OmniLearn~\cite{Mikuni:2024qsr}, vendored with the upstream OmniFold
implementation) unfolds the same ME~FHC data using, on the reconstructed
side, the actual calorimetric recoil clusters --- per-cluster energy,
one view-projected transverse coordinate and $z$ (\texttt{cluster\_energy},
\texttt{cluster\_pos}, \texttt{cluster\_z}, filtered on
\texttt{cluster\_isMuontrack} via
\texttt{CVUniverse::GetRecoClusters()}). At truth level it receives the
final-state hadron cloud with the muon removed. Muon-derived event scalars are
retained for selection and binning the extracted result, but they are not
classifier features at either level. The learned weight is therefore a
function of the recoil representation, not of the complete joint muon--hadron
event. We therefore compare its absolutely normalized central value, closure
and uncertainty only as properties of the recoil-only estimator. A full-event
estimator would instead train jointly on the declared muon and recoil inputs.

\subsection{From shape check to absolute cross section}
\label{sec:pet-abs}
The PET network trains on a GPU on a 2M-event subsample (2 OmniFold
iterations); the learned step-2 truth reweight is then applied to the
\emph{full} 32.8M-event generator sample
(\texttt{MultiFold.reweight}), binned on the 4D grid, and pushed through the
standard extraction (\texttt{extract\_cross\_section\_nd} with the GBDT
run's completeness --- completeness is reweight-independent --- and the same
flux/POT/nucleon constants). Three gates validate the absolute path:
\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Full-statistics reweight sanity}: the pushed weights over the
        32.8M generator events are finite with mean $1.028$;
  \item \textbf{Ordinary closure}: unfolding MC-reco-as-pseudo-data
        recovers MC truth to total $0.9884$ with uniform per-axis median
        residuals of $\sim\SI{1.1}{\percent}$ --- the absolute machinery is
        internally consistent. Because pseudo-data and prior come from the
        same simulation, this test cannot expose omitted muon dependence at
        fixed recoil;
  \item \textbf{Cross-check vs GBDT}: on data, the PET absolute total is
        $\SI{2.796e-38}{cm^2/nucleon}$ versus the GBDT
        $\SI{3.066e-38}{}$, ratio $\petRatio$, with per-axis median differences
        of \SIrange{6.5}{9.9}{\percent} (Fig.~\ref{fig:petabs}).
\end{enumerate}
The $\sim\SI{\petGbdtGap}{\percent}$ data-side gap is not a normalization bug,
but ordinary closure alone cannot assign it uniquely to training
configuration. The PET run used a 2M-event, two-iteration training rather than
the full-statistics five-iteration GBDT, and a higher-epoch retrain moved the
total by $-1.6\%$ (ratio $0.897$), demonstrating training sensitivity. The
recoil-only feature contract is an additional structural difference: at fixed
recoil, the conditional muon distribution remains inherited from the prior.

\subsection{Uncertainty of the recoil-only estimator}
\label{sec:pet-syst}
The PET statistical replicas independently
Poisson-fluctuate data and MC, retrain PET at a fixed estimator seed, and apply
the same coherent MC draw in the final truth binning and completeness.

The calculation uses the same background-subtracted five-dimensional
measured target as the scalar GBDT. Event-by-event data alignment is exact, the
MC arrays are byte-identical to the full-cloud input. The
nominal PET extraction, an identical-seed GPU-floor repeat, a
20-member coherent-Poisson retraining ensemble, and a 12-member crossed
subsample/estimator-seed ensemble have all passed their ordering, coverage, and
finite-output gates. The GPU nondeterminism is negligible relative to both
sampling and training variation. The $C_{\rm stat}$ estimate is based on 20
coherent replicas, so its finite-ensemble precision is more limited than the
100-replica target. These statements characterize the recoil-only estimator
and do not supply an uncertainty for a joint muon--recoil estimator.

The PET budget uses actual minus/plus endpoints with the
mean-centered MAT convention, an exact 100-universe flux inventory, and a
strict ensemble of independently data- and MC-fluctuated replicas. Every
replica retrains PET at a fixed estimator seed and carries the same coherent
MC draw through final cross-section extraction. Missing endpoints, incomplete
replica manifests, non-finite ratios, and partial event coverage are fatal.

The five-component assembly is

All matrices share the same corrected nominal and 10,550-bin mask; their sum is
finite, symmetric, and positive semidefinite. Its width-weighted 4D marginal is
also positive semidefinite and has median uncertainty
\SI{\petFourMedian}{\percent} over 4,790 reported bins.

The predeclared retraining study covered the six bands carrying
\SI{89.4}{\percent} of the preliminary vertical variance. All six showed
material map response, while an identity-ratio null retrain was only
\SI{0.008}{\percent} of the CV norm. The resulting rank-six
$C_{\rm retrain}$ is therefore retained and is the second-largest contribution
by trace. It does not double count the vertical block: the latter contains
$x_{\rm frozen}(r_u)-x_{\rm CV}$, whereas the new term contains only the
additional response $x_{\rm retrain}(r_u)-x_{\rm frozen}(r_u)$.

For detector/lateral variations, the calculation propagates shifted detector
weights and observables through the corrected nominal cloud sample with the PET
map frozen; it does not regenerate shifted-cloud membership or retrain PET for
every detector universe. The recoil-PET and GBDT median
uncertainties are of comparable scale, but their different reported masks,
central trainings and feature contracts make a binwise superiority claim
inappropriate.

\subsection{Truth-cloud projection within the recoil cross-check}
\label{sec:pet-projection}

The truth side of the recoil-only PET unfold is \emph{already} a point cloud: the
generator-level final-state hadron set
(\texttt{part\_gen}, the top-12-by-energy truth hadrons with their PDG codes,
built by \texttt{CVUniverse::GetTruthFSHadrons}) is carried per event
alongside the learned step-2 push weight. Any truth observable that is
a function of those 4-vectors can therefore be obtained after the
unfold by re-binning the push-weighted truth events --- no re-training and no
re-unfolding. We demonstrate this \emph{reweight-then-project} capability on
three observables computed post-hoc from the stored cloud: available energy
$\Eavail$, the hadronic-system invariant mass
$W_{\text{had}}=\sqrt{(\sum E)^2-|\sum\vec p|^2}$, and the proton/charged-pion
multiplicities. The exercise also quantifies the two
limitations of the stored cloud.

\paragraph{Truncation is essentially lossless.}
Because $\Eavail$ recomputed from the cloud uses the same formula and
inputs as the value stored at dump time, the cloud-vs-stored residual isolates
the cost of keeping only the 12 highest-energy hadrons. Over the full
\num{32.8}~million truth events (every event now carries a cloud, below) the
residual has median
\SI{0}{MeV} and mean \SI{\pcEavailMean}{MeV}, with \SI{\pcEavailWithin}{\percent}
of events within \SI{10}{MeV} (Fig.~\ref{fig:pcvalid}). The entire deficit
lives in the \SI{\pcSaturated}{\percent} of events that saturate the 12-particle
cap (median deficit \SI{\pcSatDeficit}{MeV} there, exactly zero below it), and
because the ranking is by energy the dropped constituents are the softest
mesons --- a small, one-sided, bounded effect. The 12-particle cap therefore
does not limit $\Eavail$-scale observables and does not need to be raised.

\paragraph{Coverage: closing the acceptance gap.}
Originally the cloud was filled only in the reco-signal event loop, so the
truth-only ``miss'' events appended for the unfold (\SI{\pcMiss}{\percent} of
the truth sample) carried no cloud --- only \SI{\pcHasCloudBefore}{\percent} of
truth events had one, and cloud projections were correspondingly
acceptance-limited, landing exactly on the stored-$\Eavail$ projection
restricted to the has-cloud subset but sitting $1.5$--$2\times$ below
the full unfold per bin. This is a truth-side instance of the same
dangling-miss-row dump issue tracked for the systematic bank. We fixed it at
source by carrying the truth final-state hadrons through the
truth-denominator loop and filling \texttt{part\_gen} for the appended miss
rows (the reco cloud \texttt{part\_reco} correctly stays empty for a miss),
then re-dumping, rebuilding the inputs, and re-training. Coverage is now
complete: \SI{\pcHasCloud}{\percent} of truth events carry a cloud (only
\pcEmptyCloud{} residual empties out of \num{32.8}~million), and the
cloud-recomputed $d\sigma/d\Eavail$ now reproduces the full unfold to
$\lesssim\SI{\pcCloudFull}{\percent}$ in every $\Eavail$ bin
(Fig.~\ref{fig:pcxsec}, center, where the cloud, stored, and full-unfold
curves coincide). The projection is no longer acceptance-limited.

\paragraph{Exploratory projection yields new hadronic diagnostics.}
$W_{\text{had}}$ is not a lossy copy of the leptonic ``experimenter's''
$W$ stored at dump time --- the latter is reconstructed from muon kinematics
assuming a single struck nucleon at rest, while $W_{\text{had}}$ is the true
invariant mass of the hadron system. The two differ by a median
\SI{\pcWoffset}{GeV}, and the 2D correlation
(Fig.~\ref{fig:pcxsec}, left) makes the physics explicit: a sharp locus at
$W_{\text{had}}\approx\SI{0.94}{GeV}$ (single-baryon final states, whose
hadronic mass is just the nucleon rest mass irrespective of leptonic $W$) and
diagonal bands stepped above it by $\sim$one nucleon mass per extra final-state
baryon (multi-nucleon knockout, 2p2h), Fermi-smeared. Projecting the cloud
thus provides a physically interpretable hadron-cloud diagnostic from the
existing recoil-only unfold; it is not promoted here as a separate full-event
measurement.

\paragraph{The projection and extrapolation limits.}
\label{sec:pet-projection-next}
Reweighting can only redistribute weight among truth configurations the
generator already produced; it cannot populate phase space the prior never
sampled --- the same extrapolation limit flagged for the extended-fiducial
study (\S\ref{sec:fps}). Within that limit, hadron-cloud diagnostics can be
projected over the stored truth spectrum. They do not
turn the recoil-derived weight into a full-event weight or remove the prior
conditional for omitted muon information.
Two extensions lie beyond re-projection from the stored cloud (they would
require regenerating the event dump):
adding the muon through separately declared reco/data and truth event-feature
interfaces (it is excluded from the recoil cloud but retained as a stored
scalar); and raising the 12-particle cap for the rare
very-high-multiplicity tail (unnecessary at $\Eavail$ scale per the validation
above). Full details and reproduction are in
\texttt{nd-unfolding/pet/POINTCLOUD\_PROJECTION.md}.

\subsection{Scope of the recoil-PET cross-check}
The cross-check establishes that the point-cloud infrastructure works on
real MINERvA recoil data: paired reco/truth clouds, GPU training, full-sample
reweighting, absolute extraction, coherent estimator bootstraps and targeted
response studies all operate end to end. It also provides a useful comparison
against the scalar result and supports exploratory post-hoc hadron-cloud
projections. It does \emph{not} establish simultaneous muon-plus-hadron
unfolding, because the muon was not a classifier input.

A full-event extension requires separate observable
\texttt{event\_reco}/\texttt{event\_data} and truth-level
\texttt{event\_truth} interfaces containing a distinguished muon, explicit
masks/types and validated neighborhood geometry. An omitted-variable stress
closure that changes muon kinematics within narrow recoil strata would test
whether the joint estimator recovers information that the recoil-only
estimator necessarily misses. Any such estimator requires its own nominal fit
and uncertainty budget.
