# Novelty-per-round — the "limit" and saturation

Does more prompting keep producing new content? **The limit is set by the QUESTION's remaining depth, not a fixed
round number.**

## Observed novelty curves
- **Shallow (near-converged) question** — the interpretation-neutrality question: value peaked R3 (adversarial:
  4 cracks) + R4 (triage), then **saturated by R5 and turned to overreach** (a false "closure" resting on a
  mis-cited ref). Beyond ~5 rounds, verification cost > marginal insight.
- **Deep (genuinely open) question** — the soft-sector crux at MAX reasoning: novelty *rose* into R2 (the decisive
  affine-weight argument) and stayed high through R3. A hard question sustains rising novelty across rounds.
- **Theorem-shaped residual (marginal floor)** — 3 independent methods; the decisive resolution (modular
  thermal-noise floor) came from one method and *refuted* another in a single round each; the moderator's numeric
  verification closed it. Depth, not round count, governed.

## Saturation signals — STOP here, then verify
(a) the contrarian **concedes**; (b) all camps converge on the **same** residual sub-question; (c) new turns become
"propose the theorem that would settle it" rather than new arguments. When all three hit (as in the edge-mode panel
R3), the next round would only restate — stop and run the verification pass.

## Recipe
Run until the saturation signals fire (not to a preset round count); match reasoning effort to depth (MAX for
frontier); then STOP and verify every load-bearing claim + every citation. "Diminishing returns by round 5" was
really "diminishing DEPTH," not a round ceiling.
